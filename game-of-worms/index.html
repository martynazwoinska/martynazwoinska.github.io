For curious grown-ups: map notes and sources

Markers show selected published collection regions, not complete species ranges. “Mostly selfing” means self-fertile hermaphrodites predominate, although males and occasional outcrossing can occur.

  • Kiontke et al. (2011) — Caenorhabditis phylogeny, habitats, and tropical/temperate distributions.
  • Félix et al. (2014) — species diagnoses, including Hawaiian C. kamaaina.
  • Woodruff & Phillips (2018) — C. inopinata in Okinawan figs and fig wasps.
  • Noble et al. (2021) — global diversity and frequent selfing in C. tropicalis.
  • Wang et al. (2026 preprint) — global sampling and geographic diversity in C. tropicalis.
  • Barrière & Félix (2005) — predominant selfing and rare outcrossing in wild C. elegans.
