# Oʻahu *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* ECA789 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Oʻahu ECA789 environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative choice and current-marker caveat

Use ECA789, formerly S-05097, as the explicit representative.

ECA789 is a distributable, sequenced CaeNDR isotype-reference strain with
unusually complete public collection metadata. It was sampled from a rotting
flower on the ground along Mānoa Falls Trail, rather than merely being assigned
to Oʻahu at island scale.

The existing Game coordinate `[-157.765, 21.356]` rounds to the shared
coordinate of ECA2898, ECA2899, ECA2900, ECA2910 and ECA2920:
`[-157.7645652, 21.355658]`. Those records describe a fruit-associated sample
“on top of leaf from bird of paradise,” but provide no named trail. Their
substrate and context must not be transferred to ECA789.

ECA789’s coordinate must replace the existing marker coordinate. Public text
must describe ECA789 as one representative Oʻahu isolate, not as every Oʻahu
record.

## Current unsupported or misleading implementation

The current scene and accessories are built from general Oʻahu scenery rather
than the isolate record.

No collection evidence connects ECA789 to:

- a panoramic Koʻolau ridge view;
- repeated knife-edged ridge fins at the collection patch;
- a visible waterfall;
- a visible stream;
- a city skyline or dry urban flank;
- aquatic habitat;
- swimwear, sunglasses or an umbrella;
- rain during collection.

Mānoa Falls Trail is a real named locality, and the official trail route
includes a stream crossing, an *Eucalyptus robusta* grove and a waterfall at
its end. None of those facts establishes what was visible from the exact
collection point.

## Exact-versus-regional evidence boundary

Exact collection evidence:

- ECA789 and previous name S-05097
- Isotype-reference status
- Coordinates and 157 m elevation
- Forest landscape
- Unidentified rotting flower on the ground
- Mānoa Falls Trail locality
- Sampling and isolation by E. Andersen on 4 August 2017
- ITS2 identification
- Substrate and ambient temperatures of 22.9 °C
- Ambient humidity of 93.7%
- C-0085 collection-plate note

Trail context only:

- Mānoa Falls Trail is at the back of Mānoa Valley
- Its official route begins with a footbridge and small-stream crossing
- The route passes through an *Eucalyptus robusta* grove
- The valley has a consistently wet climate and erosion-prone trail surfaces

Use only the trail tread and elongated smooth trunks as restrained context. Do
not depict the stream, bridge or waterfall because their relationship to the
sample point is unknown.

## Environment illustration contract

Create a dedicated original SVG scene in the existing 600 × 430 viewBox.

### Camera

- Use a close, low forest-floor viewpoint.
- Preserve central play space approximately from `x=175–445`, `y=170–350`.
- Include no scenic horizon or island panorama.

### Foreground

- Place one dominant collapsed anonymous flower near the lower left.
- Build it from unequal softened petal-like lobes around a compressed dark
  centre.
- Use muted berry, ochre and brown without implying a botanical identity.
- Set it in irregular leaf litter and shallow ground compression.
- Make its placement and decay geometry explicitly illustrative.

### Middle

- Run a broad trail tread in from the lower right.
- Turn it in one strong hook around the right edge before it narrows into the
  rear grove.
- Use one asymmetrical root lip and scattered irregular litter.
- Keep the centre clear instead of filling it with water or decorative
  vegetation.

### Background

- Frame the scene with three long, smooth, unequally leaning
  *Eucalyptus robusta*-type trunks as official trail-route context.
- Keep most trunks near the outer thirds of the image.
- Close the background with layered foliage and one slim vertical light
  opening.
- Include no mountain ridge, waterfall, stream, pool, bridge or city.

### Silhouette lock

> A collapsed anonymous flower sits low at the left, opposed by a hooked trail
> bend at the right, beneath three elongated smooth trunks closing around a
> narrow vertical forest opening.

### Forbidden

- Koʻolau ridge fins or mountain panorama.
- Waterfall, stream, river, pool or wetland.
- Honolulu skyline or visible buildings.
- Bird-of-paradise association from the separate ECA2898-coordinate records.
- Identified flower species or invented reproductive anatomy.
- Visible rain or claimed collection weather.
- Trail signs, crowds, railings or identifiable private property.
- Native-status claims for contextual vegetation.
- Lei, hula, surf, “tiki,” kapa-like patterning or other cultural/tourism
  shorthand.
- Wildlife or an invented flower-associated organism.

## Accessory contract

### ECA789 fallen-flower decay chronoscope

Family ID: `eca789-fallen-flower-decay-chronoscope`

Exact basis: ECA789 came from an unidentified rotting flower on the ground. No
decay sequence, flower identity or collection apparatus is documented.

Primary: a broad eccentric clockwork disc carrying seven unequal blank
petal-shaped shutters above a shallow litter tray, with a visibly connected
gear train, separate culture vial and reversible `S-05097 / ECA789` plate.

Companion: a tall open chain-lift carrying four staggered downturned petal
shutters, with an enclosed lower specimen drawer, offset vial, exposed return
wheel and `4 AUG 2017` date dial.

Do not turn either drawing into a decorative flower, imply a measured decay
timeline or invent identifiable floral anatomy.

### ECA789 paired microclimate harmonograph

Family ID: `eca789-paired-microclimate-harmonograph`

Exact basis: CaeNDR records substrate and ambient temperatures of 22.9 °C and
ambient humidity of 93.7%.

Primary: a wide twin-sensor console with one ground-contact shoe, one raised
ventilated cage, two separate `22.9` temperature needles, a `93.7` humidity dial
and linked styluses writing onto one horizontal paper drum.

Companion: a tall asymmetrical tripod column with a lower contact bulb, offset
upper air bulb, large looped hygrometer coil, three stacked value windows and a
narrow descending trace strip.

These are educational mechanical metaphors, not reconstructions of the field
instruments or a time series. Do not add unrecorded values.

### C-0085 field-record relay

Family ID: `c0085-field-record-relay`

Exact basis: the ECA789 record notes a C-0085 collection plate. The primary
sampling study describes barcode-linked field labels used to associate
environmental and isolation data.

Primary: a long left-to-right relay bench with three clearly connected
stations: pre-labelled collection-bag frame, abstract GPS/photo data tile and
circular isolation-plate cradle. Connect them through an exposed chain drive
ending at a separate ECA789 vial and `C-0085` plaque.

Companion: a crooked vertical folding rack with an upper tag aperture, offset
field-card drum, lower circular plate cradle, descending belt, side crank and
suspended vial, plus a reversible `S-05097 / ECA789` tab.

Do not claim C-0085 was specifically the barcode rather than the
collection-plate label. Use only an abstract, non-scannable tag pattern; include
no phone interface, application branding or invented field data.
