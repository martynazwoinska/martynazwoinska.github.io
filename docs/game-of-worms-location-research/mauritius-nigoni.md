## Correct local and regional setting

The collection locality is Casela Nature Park, Cascavelle, not Le Morne.

A contemporary Mauritius government environmental report describes Casela as a tropical planted park on a hill edge and places Rempart Mountain slopes in its wider setting. These features may establish regional silhouette and spatial character, but they do not identify the exact sampling point. Casela's current institutional history may establish the park identity; current rides, facilities, animal encounters, branding and present-day extent must not be projected backward into the 2015 collection scene.

Regional sources:

- [Mauritius government Casela and Rempart context](https://environment.govmu.org/Documents/eia/eiareports/2016/1605-mont%20calme/chap3.pdf)
- [Official Casela park history](https://caselaparks.com/the-park/)
- [UNESCO Le Morne Cultural Landscape](https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/1259/)

Le Morne is an unrelated cultural landscape associated with histories of maroon resistance. It disappears from both the scene and the wardrobe.

## Accessory contract

The three accessories are original interpretive mechanisms, not documented field equipment.
