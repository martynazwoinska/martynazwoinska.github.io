## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Barro Colorado Island, Panama environment and its three paired accessories.

## Field-experiment context

Sloat et al. report that the March 2015 Barro Colorado sampling occurred
during the dry season. At each of seven forest sites, five bait arrays occupied
the corners and centre of a 7 × 7 m field area. Each array contained six
different 6 cm agar baits arranged 3 × 2 with 30 cm spacing.

The experiment’s baits were placed on 24 March and collected on 27 March 2015.
The study tested differences in bait composition and accessibility. Some bait
types contained heat-defaunated *Gustavia superba* flower slurry.

The CaeNDR QG2726 row specifically identifies its sample as an experimental
bait labelled `Cup Lutz Eq3` and describes *Gustavia superba* slurry. The
website may connect QG2726 to the bait-array experiment, but it must not invent
the undocumented meaning of `Eq3`, reconstruct an exact cup or claim that the
worm came from a naturally fallen *Gustavia* flower.

## Exact-versus-regional evidence boundary

Exact evidence:

- QG2726 strain and isotype identity
- Coordinates, forest landscape and elevation
- Collection date and experimental bait category
- `Cup Lutz Eq3` sample label and *Gustavia superba* slurry description
- L. Noble as sampler and isolator
- 18S identification and selfed line

Experiment-level evidence:

- March 2015 dry-season sampling
- Seven forest sites and five bait arrays per site
- Six baits per array in a 3 × 2 arrangement with 30 cm spacing
- Placement on 24 March and recovery on 27 March

Regional context only:

- Barro Colorado’s lowland tropical forest and research history
- The 50-hectare ForestGEO plot
- Physical monitoring from forest floor to canopy
- The Lutz Stream weir and runoff instruments
- Muted reddish soil associated broadly with Barro Colorado

The QG2726 record does not establish that the bait was inside the 50-hectare
plot, beside the Lutz weir, visible from the research station or within view of
Gatún Lake or canal traffic.

## Accessory contract

### QG2726 Gustavia-bait array

Family ID: `qg2726-gustavia-bait-array`

Scientific basis: QG2726 came from one experimental *Gustavia superba* slurry
bait within the March 2015 Barro Colorado field experiment.

Primary: a broad low 3 × 2 array table with six unequal bait wells on visible
30 cm spacing rails, irregular forest-contact base, one highlighted QG2726
chamber and a separate culture vial in a sliding cradle.

Companion: a tall cutaway single-bait cabinet with a shallow slurry window,
separate forest-floor contact drawer, hanging sample cup and small three-step
field progression rail.

Do not add undocumented chemistry, mesh size or temperature. The pair must not
become two differently sized Petri dishes.

### BCI forest-census mapper

Family ID: `bci-forest-census-mapper`

Regional basis: Barro Colorado’s 50-hectare plot established the long-running
ForestGEO forest-census model. This object represents regional research
history, not QG2726’s collection apparatus or collection point.

Primary: a wide marquetry map table with abstract rectangular census grid,
unequal blank stem discs, sliding measurement bridge and fold-out blank census
roll.

Companion: a tall folding tree-caliper cabinet with two asymmetric sliding
jaws, stacked blank tag drawers, vertical mapping reel and separate
growth-wheel counter.

Do not reproduce real plot data, tree positions, tag numbers, logos or maps.

### Lutz runoff recorder

Family ID: `lutz-runoff-recorder`

Regional basis: STRI’s physical monitoring programme uses a 120-degree V-notch
weir, strip-chart recorder and bubble-flow instrument in the Lutz catchment.
The QG2726 sample label contains `Lutz`, but the reviewed public records do not
prove that the bait was beside the weir.

Primary: a broad 120-degree V-notch weir model with low stilling basin,
connected float well, side-mounted winding strip-chart drum and visible
mechanical linkage.

Companion: a tall bubble-flow recorder column with coiled sampling tube,
five-minute stepping wheel, separate calibration staff and narrow vertical
chart.

This is place-based research context. It must not imply that QG2726 was
aquatic, sampled from a stream or isolated with hydrological equipment.
