# Mahahual *C. nigoni* location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the *Caenorhabditis nigoni* Mahahual landscape and its three accessory pairs. It replaces an unsupported underwater reef scene with JU2617's documented rural-garden citrus heap.

## Evidence boundaries

### JU2617 collection record

The maintained Worldwide Worms/Félix entry records:

- species and strain: *Caenorhabditis nigoni* JU2617;
- previous name: Y2.1;
- locality: Mahahual, Quintana Roo, Mexico;
- coordinates: 18.72 N, -87.71 W;
- landscape: rural garden;
- substrate: rotting fruit on the ground;
- substrate comment: citrus, orange;
- note: a rotting-fruit heap, mostly oranges;
- inbreeding state: isofemale;
- identification: test cross;
- sampler: Luc Barre;
- isolator: M.-A. Félix.

Associated organism, life stage, population size, males, temperature, moisture, elevation and photograph are unreported. The current Game marker is already in correct longitude/latitude order.

The structured date says 31 December 2013 while the free-text note says January 2014. Public copy must not publish an unreconciled exact date. The note says `Yucatan`, which is treated as peninsula-level wording rather than the state because the structured locality is Quintana Roo. JU2618 was found about 100 m away in different red mango-like fruit; its substrate must not be transferred to JU2617.

Primary sources:

### Correct regional setting

The existing underwater reef, submerged seagrass and mangrove-root foreground are not the collection habitat and are retired.

Mahahual's municipal planning document describes low coastal dunes, mangroves, savannas, disturbed land, sandy ground and limestone outcrops. It supports restrained low-dune vegetation around an anonymised garden, not an exact reconstructed property. A small Mahahual lighthouse may provide regional orientation; the Mexican Navy guide documents a white 22 m truncated-pyramid tower, four white flashes every 16 seconds and a 17-nautical-mile geographic range.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects the exact mostly-orange fruit heap to one continuous anonymised low coastal garden. Marine context remains a narrow distant orientation cue rather than the collection habitat.

### Foreground: exact citrus-heap microhabitat

- A dominant asymmetric heap of visibly collapsed oranges, torn peel and softened pulp lies on garden ground.
- Individual forms differ in collapse, peel and exposure rather than repeating perfect orange circles.
- Surrounding sand, litter and stones are restrained illustrative context; no temperature, moisture or decomposition measurement is invented.

### Middle ground: anonymised coastal garden

- An uneven garden edge and curved sandy path lead through unequal low coastal-dune shrubs.
- Rounded sea-grape-like leaves and one small fan-palm cue may appear as regional flora, never sampled plants.
- No identifiable house, address, crop row or exact property plan appears.

### Background: Mahahual lock silhouette

- A very narrow ruler-flat Caribbean opening and tiny white truncated-pyramid lighthouse sit behind the garden.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is a collapsed citrus heap below unequal round-leaved dune scrub, opposed by one tiny white lighthouse against a flat horizon.
- There is no coral, submerged seagrass, reef layer, mangrove-root foreground, cruise ship, pier, cenote or Maya motif.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate the exact citrus decay, recorded identification method and factual regional lighthouse into movable teaching objects. They are original mechanisms, not claimed field equipment.

### JU2617 citrus-heap respiration recorder

- **Primary:** a broad low bell jar containing unequal collapsed citrus forms, connected by a capillary to a bubble wheel and horizontal trace drum, with reversible `JU2617 / Y2.1` plate.
- **Companion:** a tall stacked peel-and-pulp chamber inside a U-tube frame, with bead counter, side culture vial and lower litter drawer.
- The drawing shows no invented temperature, humidity or respiration value.

### JU2617 test-cross lock

The record supports `test cross`, but not tester identity, parental strain, result or progeny count.

- **Primary:** a wide X-arm semaphore with two blank culture wells, central observation window and sliding diagnostic shutters.
- **Companion:** a tall offset twin-tube gate tower feeding a vertical observation column with blank progeny windows.
- This is the second and final test-cross semantic family after JU4400. Its X-arm cabinet and vertical gate remain visibly unlike JU4400's bridge and circular dial.

### Mahahual lighthouse flash sequencer

- **Primary:** a tall truncated-pyramid tower mechanism with four lantern shutters and a 16-second timing ring.
- **Companion:** a low circular Fresnel-style sequencing drum with four flash apertures, escapement wheel and curved base, with no miniature tower.
- The drawings are original factual abstractions and use no Navy logo or copied institutional detail.

## Cultural, privacy and ecological safeguards

- Do not identify a private garden from coarse coordinates.
- Do not introduce Maya motifs, cenotes, tourist branding or national-colour costume shorthand.
- Do not place JU2617 underwater or imply that it came from a reef, seagrass or mangrove sample.
- Keep JU2618's different fruit and sample separate.

## Quality benchmark

The revised preceding locations are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. Mahahual must exceed them through an exact citrus focal point, coherent low-garden depth, integrated mechanisms, two genuinely different drawings per concept and a readable combined composition.
