# Edinburgh location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the Edinburgh landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the Bristol N2 and Santeuil batches in the location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow.

## Evidence boundaries

### Representative collection record

The current Game marker says only `Edinburgh, Scotland` at 55.92, -3.19. The official current CaeNDR data place twelve strains from four isotype groups at those rounded coordinates. All are recorded as urban-garden compost samples, so the marker must not be presented as one uniquely identified isolate.

This illustration uses **ED3010** as an explicit representative because it matches the marker and has a precise allotment record:

- species: *Caenorhabditis elegans*;
- strain: ED3010, in the ED3012 isotype group;
- coordinates: 55.92, -3.19;
- landscape: urban garden;
- substrate: compost in a compost bin;
- site: Midmar Allotment, field 1, plot 39;
- sampler and isolator: A. Cutter.

The current CaeNDR file dates ED3010 to 26 November 2004. The CGC catalogue displays 2005, but the primary paper containing ED3010 was received in July 2005, before a November 2005 collection could have taken place. The artwork does not print a year; the dossier follows CaeNDR for the current structured date and uses CGC only for the field-and-plot detail.

Sources:

- [CaeNDR current *C. elegans* strain data](https://caendr.org/request-strains/download/c_elegans/latest/strain-data/csv)
- [CaeNDR ED3012 isotype group](https://caendr.org/isotype/ED3012/)
- [Caenorhabditis Genetics Center Edinburgh strain entries](https://cgc.umn.edu/species/caenorhabditis%20elegans?f=1&offset=4550)
- [Cutter, 2006, Nucleotide Polymorphism and Linkage Disequilibrium in Wild Populations of *C. elegans*](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC1456145/)

Cutter's study sampled multiple Edinburgh-area sites, including different Midmar plots, West Mains and mushroom-farm compost near North Berwick. The Game scene is explicitly ED3010's Midmar compost-bin setting. It must not generalise mushroom compost or another Edinburgh site's details to this representative.

### Correct local setting

The rounded Game marker lies beside Midmar Allotments and Blackford Hill, not at Holyrood Park. The existing Arthur's Seat, Salisbury Crags and loch concept therefore mislocates the collection context and is retired.

- The City of Edinburgh Council lists Midmar as an official allotment site.
- The Council's allotment strategy records communal compost bays, a large compost tumbler and demonstration raised beds at Midmar. These are later site features and local design references, not proof that ED3010 came from the tumbler itself.
- The nearby Hermitage of Braid and Blackford Hill reserve combines mixed grassland, scrub, woodland, Braid Burn and a low volcanic hill.
- The Edinburgh Geological Society describes Blackford Hill's purple andesite and its asymmetric crag-and-tail form: steeper to the west and gentler to the east.
- The Royal Observatory Edinburgh crowns Blackford Hill with a long red-sandstone building, octagonal end towers and two cylindrical green-copper telescope housings.

Reference pages:

- [Midmar Allotments, City of Edinburgh Council](https://www.edinburghoutdoors.org.uk/directory-record/323/midmar)
- [Edinburgh allotment strategy 2010–2015](https://www.edinburgh.gov.uk/downloads/file/22647/allotment-strategy-2010-2015)
- [Blackford Hill and pond, City of Edinburgh Council](https://www.edinburghoutdoors.org.uk/directory-record/9/blackford-hill-and-pond)
- [Blackford Hill geology, Edinburgh Geological Society](https://edinburghgeolsoc.org/geological-site/blackford-hill/)
- [Royal Observatory Edinburgh heritage](https://www.roe.ac.uk/roe/heritage/index.html)

These pages are factual and visual references only. The website uses an original SVG illustration and does not trace an external photograph, map or institutional graphic.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects one exact compost-bin microhabitat to the immediately neighbouring allotment and Blackford landscape. It must not become a generic Edinburgh skyline or a row of garden symbols.

### Foreground: recorded microhabitat

- An open timber compost bin is the closest and most legible object.
- Its layers show decomposing green waste, brown leaves, stems and damp organic matter without turning the pile into rubbish or a pristine flower bed.
- An optional small `1/39` plot stake may identify the representative record without becoming a decorative title.
- Raised-bed boards enter from unequal angles and lead the eye into the allotment.

### Middle ground: Midmar allotment

- Irregular plots, modest sheds, hedges and winter or late-season planting form one receding garden space.
- Bed edges converge toward Blackford Hill instead of forming repeated horizontal field strips.
- Compost remains more prominent than regional landmarks.

### Background: Blackford lock silhouette

- Blackford Hill is a low asymmetric crag-and-tail landform, with a steeper western face and a longer gentler tail.
- The Royal Observatory sits on the crest as a long red-sandstone bar with separated green cylindrical housings.
- Arthur's Seat, Salisbury Crags, a loch and the old generic city skirt do not appear.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is an angular compost bay and converging plots beneath one lopsided low hill topped by two separated green drums.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate the representative microhabitat and nearby scientific landscape into movable play objects. They are not claims about Edinburgh dress.

### Midmar compost tumbler

The Council documents a large tumbler at Midmar, but it post-dates the strain record. It is a locally grounded compost mechanism, not the claimed source of ED3010.

- **Primary:** tall faceted drum in a stable A-frame; side crank; vent rows; inspection hatch; restrained cutaway showing different brown and green compost layers.
- **Companion:** low slatted barrel in a crescent cradle; end handwheel; two differently placed inspection doors; distinct footing and rotational axis.
- Neither drawing may look like N2's jetpack, an ordinary backpack or a recoloured cylinder.

### GALAXY plate-scanner engine

The Royal Observatory's GALAXY machine scanned astronomical photographic plates and preceded SuperCOSMOS. It provides a locally scientific object that is neither another telescope nor a generic computer.

Sources:

- [Royal Observatory Edinburgh: SuperCOSMOS facility](https://www.roe.ac.uk/ifa/wfau/cosmos/facility.html)
- [Royal Observatory Edinburgh history](https://www.roe.ac.uk/roe/history.html)
- [National Museums Scotland GALAXY/COSMOS scanner entry](https://www.nms.ac.uk/collections/search/object?creator=Sogenique%20%28Service%29%20Ltd&entry=703800&page=1&view=cards)

Required construction:

- **Primary:** wide cast-iron rectangular frame; square star plate on a moving carriage; vertical scanning head; control wheel, counter and cable or guide detail.
- **Companion:** tall narrow portal frame; diagonally mounted plate carriage; offset scan head; different counter and foot geometry.
- The star image is a restrained plate clue, not a copied survey image or institutional logo.

### Agassiz ice-flow model

Agassiz Rock near Blackford Hill records the history of recognising glacial striations around Edinburgh. The accessory is an educational replica; worms must not be shown scraping, collecting or altering protected rock.

Source: [Agassiz Rock geological account, Scottish Geology Trust geoguide](https://geoguide.scottishgeologytrust.org/p/gcr06/gcr06_agassizrock).

- **Primary:** horizontal purple-andesite relief slab with parallel grooves; translucent ice carriage moving eastward on a brass rail; direction and motion shown by geometry rather than explanatory text.
- **Companion:** upright crag-and-tail cutaway theatre; steep west face, gentle east tail and hand-cranked ice ribbon; distinct silhouette and mechanism.
- The purple andesite and ice colours are scientifically meaningful and may differ from the general site palette.

## Quality benchmark

The revised Bristol N2 and Santeuil batches are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. Edinburgh must exceed them through a defensible exact setting, coherent allotment-to-hill depth, constructed materials, different primary/companion mechanisms and a readable combined composition.

## Review and interaction acceptance

- Only the Edinburgh landscape and Edinburgh accessory data/drawings change in the Game feature batch.
- The public marker remains `Edinburgh, Scotland`, while explanatory copy calls ED3010 representative.
- Every concept has a dedicated SVG drawing path; none uses a generic sampler, accordion or fallback renderer.
- The old ear-warmers, contour accordion, loch sampler, pond, Arthur's Seat and Salisbury Crags are absent.
- Primary and companion copies differ in outline, construction, internal detail and placement.
- All three pairs remain legible together and do not hide worm faces.
- Each of the six visible objects remains independently draggable and keyboard movable; Home resets only the focused copy.
- Accessible names match the visible objects.
- Pointer and keyboard tests cover all six copies.
- Visual checks cover light and dark themes and responsive widths from 360 px to wide desktop.
- No horizontal overflow, console error, broken source link or change to another location is accepted.
- Environment/accessory audits, syntax checks, `git diff --check` and the full diff pass before integration.
