# Pohnpei *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* QG4739 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Pohnpei QG4739 environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative choice and current-marker audit

Use QG4739, previously C-0230F, as the explicit representative.

QG4739 is:

- an isotype-reference strain;
- whole-genome sequenced;
- distributable through CaeNDR;
- recorded without a strain issue;
- supported by exact coordinates, locality, habitat, substrate, temperatures,
  collection date, collectors, isolators and species-identification method.

QG4739 is not the only strain from its collection coordinate. The current
CaeNDR release contains seven separately named isotype-reference strains at
6.9066, 158.1818 with the same date, substrate, temperatures and collection
team:

| Previous name | Current strain |
| --- | --- |
| C-0230A | QG5588 |
| C-0230B | QG4780 |
| C-0230C | QG4759 |
| C-0230D | QG5161 |
| C-0230E | QG5104 |
| C-0230F | QG4739 |
| C-0230G | QG5405 |

No reported field makes one of these seven scientifically superior. QG4739 is
a pragmatic stable representative with a live direct isotype record, not a
claim of priority or uniqueness. Public copy must describe it as one
representative Pohnpei isolate.

The current Game coordinate `[158.182, 6.907]` is a rounded match to this
cluster. Moving it to `[158.1818, 6.9066]` changes the point by only about
49.7 m.

## Botanical-name boundary

The CaeNDR substrate comment must be preserved verbatim when discussing the
record: `Clinostigma ponapensis - Kotop`.

Kew Plants of the World Online accepts *Clinostigma ponapense* and records it
as a tree native to Pohnpei in the wet tropical biome. The College of
Micronesia flora also uses *C. ponapense* and records `kotop` as its Pohnpeian
name.

Do not silently rewrite the CaeNDR field as though CaeNDR used the accepted
spelling. Public explanatory copy may say:

> CaeNDR records the substrate as *Clinostigma ponapensis*, locally named
> kotop; Kew currently accepts the botanical name *Clinostigma ponapense*.

## Why the current implementation is unsupported

The current profile is `pohnpei-basalt-mangrove`, titled `Pohnpei's volcanic
island and tidal ruins`. It depicts or names:

- Nan Madol;
- basalt-and-coral walls;
- tidal canals;
- mangroves;
- lagoon water;
- a steep island panorama;
- a waterfall;
- rain;
- basalt cuffs;
- a lagoon snorkel;
- basalt-log chimes.

UNESCO documents Nan Madol as more than 100 constructed islets off Pohnpei's
south-east coast, with basalt and coral walls and waterways affected by
mangrove growth. That description is valid for Nan Madol but not for QG4739.

Using the UNESCO listed point, Nan Madol is approximately 18.1 km in a
straight line from the QG4739 coordinate. More importantly, no CaeNDR field
connects QG4739 to Nan Madol, basalt architecture, coral, tidal canals,
mangroves, lagoon water or a waterfall.

The collection record instead identifies Paies, Kitti cloudforest, a rotting
kotop-palm fruit substrate and an elevation of approximately 540–542 m.

Therefore:

- the Nan Madol scene is not merely imprecise; it represents a different
  environment and a culturally significant site unsupported by the strain
  record;
- the mangrove, lagoon and snorkel are coastal imagery incompatible with the
  recorded cloudforest collection;
- the waterfall is unsupported generic island imagery;
- the basalt cuffs and basalt-log chimes turn unrelated heritage architecture
  into costume and instrument motifs.

The scene and all three accessory families must be replaced.

## Environment illustration contract

Create a dedicated original SVG scene in the existing 600 × 430 viewBox.

### Camera

- Use a close, low, slightly upslope cloudforest-floor view.
- Preserve central play space approximately from `x=175–435`,
  `y=150–345`.
- Keep the scene enclosed and terrestrial.
- Include no panoramic island, coast or horizon.

### Foreground

- Place one low, irregular softened kotop-fruit sample partly nested in damp
  leaf litter near the left root lattice.
- Treat fruit count, outer shape, size, colour, degree of collapse and litter
  placement as explicitly illustrative.
- Prefer muted plum-brown, olive and ochre forms rather than bright ripe-fruit
  colour.
- A short abstract rachis fragment may connect the forms if every depicted
  element visibly belongs to the same substrate mass.
- Do not show a measured fruit size or imply that a whole infructescence was
  collected.
- Do not add basalt blocks, coral, shells, standing water or a trail sign.

### Middle

- Use one tall, pale, subtly ringed palm trunk entering from the upper-left
  edge.
- Split its base into an open asymmetric root lattice that produces strong
  negative space without covering the worms.
- Carry one long drooping pinnate frond diagonally across the upper edge, with
  fine unequal leaflet rhythm.
- These palm features are source-informed botanical context, not a
  reconstruction of the exact source tree.
- Make the forest floor one strong rising diagonal from lower left toward
  upper right.
- Add only restrained unidentified understory leaves and small fern-like
  silhouettes; do not identify another plant species.
- Keep the middle distance free of paths, stairs, walls, buildings and water.

### Background

- Close the scene with overlapping vertical cloudforest trunks and a steep
  layered slope.
- Use two or three thin pale mist bands between the trunk layers as habitat
  context.
- The mist must not read as rainfall, spray, smoke or a waterfall.
- Leave one narrow high light slit behind the upper-right slope.
- Include no mountain panorama, lagoon, coastal opening or cultural landmark.

### Silhouette lock

> One pale ringed palm column opens into an asymmetric root lattice at lower
> left, a collapsed kotop-fruit sample sits just inside it, and one steep
> diagonal cloudforest floor disappears into layered mist at upper right.

This must remain immediately distinguishable from:

- Praslin's giant fan-leaf vault, pink-grey granite cleft and boulder drainage
  thread;
- Kauaʻi QG131's pandanus canopy, prop roots, descending beach trail and
  coastal opening;
- Manaus JU1976's opposed ground-level palm fans and narrow vertical shaft;
- La Selva QG3845's broad buttress and Barva-corridor ridge slit;
- New Taipei NIC1648's zig-zag concrete stair;
- the old rectilinear Nan Madol wall silhouette.

### Forbidden

- Nan Madol, archaeological walls, basalt logs or coral blocks.
- Tidal canals, lagoon, mangrove, reef, beach, open sea or snorkelling imagery.
- Waterfall, river, stream, pool, visible rain or spray.
- Broad volcanic-island skyline or generic tropical-island panorama.
- Giant fan-shaped coco-de-mer canopy, granite cleft or Praslin drainage
  geometry.
- Pandanus prop-root or beach-trail composition.
- Invented road, trail, building, dwelling, sign or access route.
- National flags, maps, costume motifs or pseudo-traditional decoration.
- Archaeological or ceremonial imagery used as costume or prop.
- Identified neighbouring plants.
- Fruit-size labels, invented sample count or reconstructed infructescence.
- Claim that the pictured palm was the sampled tree.
- Claim that mist or precipitation was observed on the collection date.
