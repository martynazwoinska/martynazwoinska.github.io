# Singapore *C. nigoni* location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the *Caenorhabditis nigoni* Singapore landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the evidence-led, location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow.

## Evidence boundaries

### ZF1220 collection record

The current Game marker `Singapore · ZF1220` at 1.32, 103.82 matches the Félix Lab wild-isolate record:

- species: *Caenorhabditis nigoni*;
- strain: ZF1220;
- other name: SF0;
- genotype: wild isolate;
- coordinates: 1.32, 103.82;
- collection date: 3 September 2011;
- substrate: rotten starfruit;
- samplers: Adeline Seah and Takao Inoue;
- isolator: Takao Inoue;
- culture provenance: multifemale line.

The record gives no elevation, exact address, landscape class, garden, tree-versus-ground placement or weather. `Multifemale line` does not provide a founder count and must not be illustrated as a known number.

A recent NCBI ZF1220-2 assembly attaches Hong Kong and 2024 fields to its sequencing BioSample. Those cannot describe the documented original wild collection in Singapore in 2011 and must not replace the Félix provenance.

Primary sources:

- [Félix Lab ZF1220 record](https://www.justbio.com/tools/worms/details.php?strain_id=333574)
- [NCBI ZF1220-2 assembly record](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/datasets/genome/GCA_977007525.2/)

### Correct regional setting

The existing mangrove, mudflat, boardwalk, rain and broad skyline are unsupported. The rounded coordinates are inland in central Singapore. NParks describes starfruit as a well-drained terrestrial tree that does not tolerate salinity or flooding, further weakening the wetland reconstruction.

The Singapore Botanic Gardens supplies defensible regional language for a tropical scientific garden near the rounded coordinate, but the strain record does not name the Gardens. Planted tropical scenery and a narrow city gap therefore remain explicitly regional context, never the claimed collection site.

Authoritative setting sources:

- [NParks starfruit description](https://www.nparks.gov.sg/FloraFaunaWeb/Flora/2/7/2736)
- [Singapore Botanic Gardens visitor information](https://sbg.nparks.gov.sg/visit/general-info/)
- [Singapore Botanic Gardens plant collections](https://sbg.nparks.gov.sg/attractions/the-gardens/)

These pages are factual and visual references only. The website uses an original SVG illustration and does not trace an external photograph, map, private parcel or institutional graphic.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects the exact rotten-starfruit substrate to one continuous planted tropical setting and a very narrow urban opening. It must not become a mangrove scene or city tourism panorama.

### Foreground: exact starfruit microhabitat

- A dominant softened, five-ridged rotten starfruit and one detached star-shaped cross-section form the closest focal group.
- Placement in surrounding litter is illustrative because the source does not say whether the fruit was fallen, attached, discarded or sampled elsewhere.
- Collapse, softened ribs and restrained decay cues distinguish the fruit from a fresh decorative star.

### Middle ground: planted tropical inference

- One low drooping *Averrhoa carambola* bough uses pinnate leaves and restrained purple flower panicles described by NParks.
- An asymmetric planted path curves beneath the bough and into the background.
- The scene does not claim that this is the source tree or that collection occurred in a protected garden.

### Background: Singapore lock silhouette

- Unequal tropical botanical canopy opens onto only a narrow rectilinear central-city gap.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is an unmistakable five-point fruit below a drooping pinnate arch, with an S-curving path disappearing through one narrow urban opening.
- There is no mangrove, mudflat, boardwalk, coast, rain curtain or broad skyline.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate the exact fruit, database provenance and defensible regional scientific history into movable teaching objects. They are original educational mechanisms, not claimed collection or historical equipment.

### ZF1220 five-rib field atlas

- **Primary:** a broad pentagonal shutter-codex around a starfruit cross-section, with five unequal hinged folios, specimen pocket, rib and leaf diagrams and a `ZF1220 / SF0` tag.
- **Companion:** a tall spiral-spine atlas with staggered rib-shaped pages, a longitudinal fruit sleeve and sliding location tab.
- This is an educational atlas, not another avocado microhabitat viewer, fan or recoloured book.

### Multifemale provenance merger

- **Primary:** a wide many-to-one channel table with several deliberately unnumbered founder ports converging on one ZF1220 culture chamber.
- **Companion:** a circular offset merger drum whose separate inlet tracks converge into one suspended culture vial.
- The object illustrates a database term rather than a reconstructed pedigree and never prints or implies a founder count.

### Holttum orchid hybridisation engine

The Botanic Gardens history documents Eric Holttum's 1928 orchid breeding and hybridisation laboratories. The mechanism is a playful regional scientific translation, not equipment claimed to have collected ZF1220.

Source: [Singapore Botanic Gardens history](https://sbg.nparks.gov.sg/about/our-history/).

- **Primary:** a wide dual-flower pollination bench with unlike abstract orchid clamps, movable pollen bridge, seed-capsule window and blank pedigree ribbon.
- **Companion:** a tall asymmetric propagation tower with capsule hopper, separate germination flasks, parentage dial and side crank.
- The drawings do not reproduce a branded apparatus, institutional emblem or named orchid hybrid.

## Cultural, privacy and scientific safeguards

## Quality benchmark

The revised preceding locations are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. Singapore must exceed them through an exact five-ridged fruit focal point, coherent planted depth, integrated mechanisms, two genuinely different drawings per concept and a readable combined composition.
