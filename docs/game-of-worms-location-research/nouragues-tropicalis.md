# Nouragues *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* JU1428 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Nouragues JU1428 environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative choice

Use JU1428 as the explicit representative.

It is CaeNDR’s isotype-reference strain for an isotype containing JU1428,
JU1429 and JU1430; its alternative name is 4A2. The record has exact locality,
elevation and substrate information and is also maintained by the CGC.

## Representative collection record

- Species: *Caenorhabditis tropicalis*
- Strain and isotype reference: JU1428
- Other strains in the isotype: JU1429 and JU1430
- Alternative name: 4A2
- Coordinates: 4.08, -52.68
- Elevation: 86 m
- Landscape: forest
- CaeNDR substrate category: rotting nut, pod, seed or fruit
- Identified substrate: rotting *Duguetia surinamensis* fruit
- Locality: Petit Plateau, Nouragues Forest, French Guiana
- Sampled by: P. Châtelet
- Isolated by: M.-A. Félix
- Reproductive form recorded by CGC: hermaphrodite

CaeNDR encodes the sampling date as 30 April 2008, while the CGC narrative says
May 2008. Treat the exact day and month boundary as unresolved and use `in 2008`
in public Game copy.

CaeNDR reports no strain issue. Substrate temperature, ambient temperature and
humidity were not recorded.

The sources do not report whether the fruit was attached or on the ground, its
dimensions, exact decay geometry, surrounding plants, associated organisms,
weather, trail position or a collection-patch photograph.

## Botanical boundary

*Duguetia surinamensis* is an accepted wet-tropical tree native from
northeastern Colombia through the Guianas to northern Peru.

Published species descriptions characterize its mature fruit as brown or
probably red in life, globose to subglobose and composed of many partly fused
carpels. These traits can guide an identifiable aggregate-fruit silhouette.
They do not establish the colour, dimensions, intactness or carpel visibility
of the particular JU1428 fruit.

## Petit Plateau and station context

Official reserve material places Petit Plateau at the Inselberg station:

- It is a forested area with gentler slopes than Grand Plateau.
- Its average elevation is approximately 120 m; this is site context and does
  not replace CaeNDR’s strain elevation of 86 m.
- Its substrate is granite of the same geological origin as the nearby
  inselberg.
- Its soil is poorly drained, acidic and relatively poor in ferruginous
  nodules.
- The nearby Inselberg station supports plateau and high-relief forest
  research.
- The Pararé station and Inselberg station are 8 km apart.
- Pararé, not Petit Plateau, is the station site beside the Arataye River.

The reserve’s long-running litterfall programme uses 40 collectors across
Grand and Petit Plateau, checked every 15 days. Material is dried and weighed,
and a monthly 100 g sample from each plateau is sent for analysis. This supports
one regional research accessory but is not part of JU1428’s collection
procedure.

## Exact-versus-regional evidence boundary

Exact collection evidence:

- JU1428 and its CaeNDR isotype-reference status
- Alternative name 4A2
- JU1429 and JU1430 in the same isotype
- Coordinates and 86 m elevation
- Forest landscape
- Rotting *Duguetia surinamensis* fruit
- Petit Plateau locality
- Sampling by P. Châtelet and isolation by M.-A. Félix in 2008

Regional or site context only:

- Petit Plateau’s gentle forested terrain
- Poorly drained acidic soil over granite
- Nearby granitic inselberg
- Dense humid Amazonian forest
- The long-term litterfall monitoring programme
- General Nouragues findings that *Caenorhabditis* occurs much more often in
  rotting fruit than ordinary litter

No source shows that JU1428 was collected beside a river, in a canoe-access
clearing, near a station building, on bare inselberg rock, in visible rain or
with an open view of the inselberg.

## Environment illustration contract

Create a dedicated original SVG scene in the existing 600 × 430 viewBox.

### Camera

- Use a close, low forest-floor viewpoint.
- Emphasize the documented fruit substrate and gentle plateau ground.
- Preserve an uncluttered central play space for both worms and all six movable
  objects.
- Do not use a river-approach camera.

### Foreground

- Place one dominant softened *Duguetia* aggregate fruit near the lower left.
- Give it an irregular globose outline with many shallow, unequal fused carpel
  panels.
- Use muted brown, oxblood and dull red rather than a bright pristine fruit.
- Partly compress one side to communicate decay without inventing a split
  pattern or exposed seeds.
- Place it in irregular leaf litter, explicitly as an illustrative placement.
- Add no sample flag, label, worm, wildlife or apparatus.

### Middle

- Shape the ground as a broad, gentle plateau rather than a ridge or valley.
- Use a shallow crescent-shaped dark damp hollow as restrained context for
  poorly drained soil; it must not read as a stream.
- Suggest the granite substrate with two small broken stone edges partly buried
  beneath soil, not a bare-rock field.
- Frame the central opening with two unequal trunks leaning outward, plus
  differently directed roots.
- Keep the central ground visually quiet and traversable.

### Background

- Close most of the horizon with layered humid forest.
- Leave one narrow far-right opening containing only a cropped pale granite
  shoulder of the nearby inselberg.
- The rock shoulder is a regional site cue, not a reconstructed view from the
  collection patch.
- Do not show the complete dome or rock-savanna vegetation.

### Silhouette lock

> A knobbled, softened aggregate fruit sits beside a shallow crescent-shaped
> plateau hollow, framed by two outward-leaning trunks and opposed by one narrow
> cropped granite shoulder inside otherwise closed forest.

### Forbidden

- Arataye River, riverbank, broad water band, canoe or pirogue.
- Pararé camp or a station building.
- A dominant full inselberg dome or scenic panorama.
- Bare rock savanna presented as the collection habitat.
- Falling-rain curtain or claimed collection weather.
- A pristine attached fruit or reconstructed *Duguetia* tree.
- Invented seeds, insects, primates or dispersal association.
- Exact trail, plot grid or property reconstruction.
- Flags or unrelated cultural motifs.

## Accessory contract

### JU1428 Duguetia fruit theatre

Family ID: `ju1428-duguetia-fruit-theatre`

Exact basis: JU1428 was isolated from rotting *Duguetia surinamensis* fruit.
Species-level fruit morphology supports an aggregate, many-carpelled form, but
the sampled fruit’s exact appearance is unknown.

Primary: a broad low asymmetric theatre whose two hinged many-carpel shell
wings open around a central specimen cradle; include a separate culture vial,
exposed crankshaft, connected hinge rods and reversible `4A2 / JU1428` plate.

Companion: a tall open carpel elevator with staggered ovoid drawers around a
central rail, a separate lower fruit cradle, side-mounted vial and offset
`JU1428` tab.

The companion must not be a rotated or narrowed version of the primary. Do not
label a carpel count, specimen dimensions, colour stage or seed number.

### Nouragues litterfall chronobalance

Family ID: `nouragues-litterfall-chronobalance`

Regional basis: the official long-term programme uses 40 litter collectors
across Grand and Petit Plateau, checked every 15 days; material is dried and
weighed and monthly 100 g subsamples are sent for analysis. It is not JU1428
collection equipment.

Primary: a broad suspended balance beneath an asymmetric botanical arch, with
three differently shaped catch-net baskets, one connected 15-day escapement
wheel, drying rack, counterweight and small `PP` plateau tab.

Companion: a tall drying-and-weighing tower with one raised litter tray, an
offset hanging balance, separate 100 g sample drawer, vertical 15-day ratchet
and exposed return cable.

Do not draw 40 repeated miniature baskets, invent biomass values or imply that
the programme collected JU1428.

### JU1428 isotype-triad comparator

Family ID: `ju1428-isotype-triad-comparator`

Exact basis: CaeNDR groups JU1428, JU1429 and JU1430 in the JU1428 isotype and
lists 4A2 as its alternative name.

Primary: a wide triangular three-vial comparison bench, with separate `1428`,
`1429` and `1430` chambers connected to one central isotype lens, a restrained
`4A2` plate and one hand-driven matching shutter.

Companion: a tall rotating three-drawer spine with vertically offset strain
chambers, one independent viewing gate, lower winding drum and side-mounted
`4A2` medallion.

Do not show nucleotide sequences, variant counts, identity percentages or
claim that the three strains are interchangeable clones.
