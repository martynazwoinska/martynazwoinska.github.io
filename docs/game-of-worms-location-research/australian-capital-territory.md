# Australian Capital Territory location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the Australian Capital Territory landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the Bristol N2, Santeuil, Edinburgh, Tenerife and *C. elegans* Kauaʻi batches in the location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow.

## Evidence boundaries

### Representative collection record

The current Game marker says `Australian Capital Territory` at -35.254, 149.115. Current CaeNDR data identify **QG2811** as a strong explicit representative that matches this rounded marker:

- species: *Caenorhabditis elegans*;
- strain and isotype reference: QG2811;
- same isotype and sample: QG2810;
- coordinates: -35.2542, 149.1151;
- elevation: 590 m;
- landscape: urban garden;
- substrate category: rotting nut, pod, seed or fruit;
- substrate comment: figs;
- locality: a backyard on Nardoo Crescent, Canberra, ACT;
- sampler and isolator: M. Rockman.

CaeNDR's structured sampling date is 2 April 2017. Its notes say the figs were collected on 22 March; QG2811 was derived from a hermaphrodite recovered through a Baermann funnel on 2 April, while QG2810 came from the same figs placed directly on a plate. The artwork may distinguish these stages, but must not silently collapse them into one date.

The fig species or cultivar, source tree, exact position in the garden, garden layout, weather, moisture, mould species and surrounding plants are not recorded. The public artwork must not name the homeowner, reproduce an identifiable house or imply that its anonymised garden is an exact reconstruction.

### Correct local and regional setting

The existing field quadrat and Lake Burley Griffin scene materially mislocate an urban-backyard fig sample. Both disappear.

Nardoo Crescent is in O'Connor. Nearby O'Connor Ridge protects dry forest and Yellow Box–Blakely's Red Gum woodland, so a low dry ridge and open eucalypt crown are defensible neighbourhood context. An official ACT register documents a Yellow Box on Nardoo Crescent, but it does not prove that the sampled garden contained that tree. Black Mountain may appear only as a tiny regional orientation cue; it is not the collection habitat and may not have been visible from the yard.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects the documented rotting-fig microhabitat to an anonymised backyard and restrained neighbourhood context. It must not become an identifiable property, generic reserve or lakeside panorama.

### Foreground: exact fig microhabitat

- Two differently collapsed rotting figs form the closest focal pair.
- One may split to reveal an anonymous seed-speckled interior; the other remains sagged and partly intact.
- Muted, non-cultivar-specific colours and irregular softened outlines distinguish decay without inventing a visible mould species.
- A curved decay-and-litter patch leads into the garden rather than becoming another circular specimen dish.

### Middle ground: anonymised urban garden

- Unequal garden edging, stepping stones and a low irregular fence or roof fragment establish a backyard without identifying a real property.
- One open-crowned Yellow Box-like tree may reference the street and nearby woodland, explicitly as neighbourhood context.
- The garden is one continuous space, not a row of eucalyptus symbols, suburban icons or pristine flower beds.

### Background: O'Connor lock silhouette

- Low dry O'Connor Ridge woodland closes the view.
- At most, a hairline offset Black Mountain tower provides regional orientation.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is an asymmetric pair of collapsed figs below a broken garden edge, one broad open eucalypt crown and a low ridge with a tiny tower.
- There is no lake, field quadrat, waterside reed band or pretence that the sample came from a nature reserve.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate the exact recovery method, neighbourhood ecology and regional scientific landmark into movable teaching objects. They use no Indigenous cultural motifs, Australian-animal stereotypes or national-colour costume shorthand.

### QG2811 Baermann fig-recovery rig

The exact notes make a Baermann apparatus the strongest scientific object. The later field protocol co-authored by M. Rockman provides construction context: a filtered sample sits in water above a sloping funnel and clamped outlet.

Source: [Crombie et al., 2022, Field sampling and laboratory isolation of *Caenorhabditis* nematodes](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8857960/).

- **Primary:** a tall glass funnel in a complete brass ring stand; mesh-and-tissue hammock holding irregular fig material; visible water line; connected rubber tube, functional clamp and receiving vial marked QG2811.
- **Companion:** a squat tripod extractor with a broad sloped transparent reservoir, central suspended sample basket, coiled side tube, wheel clamp and offset collection vial.
- The constructions differ vertically versus horizontally, single-column versus three-leg support, straight versus coiled tubing and integrated versus offset vial placement.
- Every rod visibly connects to a ring, clamp, foot or reservoir; no unexplained sticks are accepted.

### Yellow Box seed orrery

This playful dispersal machine is grounded in documented neighbourhood vegetation. It is not made from collected protected material and is not proof that QG2811 was found below a Yellow Box.

- **Primary:** a wide radial brass mechanism centred on one woody capsule cutaway, with six unequal articulated branch arms, lanceolate-leaf counterweights, seed-release shutters and a winding key.
- **Companion:** a tall two-capsule gravity column with different star-shaped valves, a leaf-vane escapement and crescent seed-catching tray.
- It replaces simplistic gum-nut maracas and must read as a coherent mechanism rather than seedpods attached to sticks.
- It does not reuse Tenerife's rectangular Linnaean seed-exchange engine.

### Black Mountain signal theremin

This is an original playful translation of Canberra's telecommunications landmark, not a traditional instrument or a traced tower.

- **Primary:** a tall tapered mast above a curved soundbox, with one pitch rod, one horizontal volume loop, three restrained signal rings and a waveform gauge.
- **Companion:** a low crescent-bodied electronic instrument with two crossed horizontal antenna loops, sliding frequency scale, offset speaker grille and short side mast.
- Neither drawing uses Telstra branding, and the two worms do not receive the same tower silhouette.

## Cultural and privacy safeguards

- Do not identify or reconstruct the private backyard or publish the homeowner's name.
- O'Connor Ridge is Ngunnawal Country; no cultural form is turned into generic costume decoration.
- Do not depict protected vegetation as harvested material.
- Do not introduce kangaroos, koalas, flags or other national shorthand in place of the exact record.

## Quality benchmark

The revised Bristol N2, Santeuil, Edinburgh, Tenerife and Kauaʻi batches are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. The ACT scene must exceed them through an exact fig-and-recovery focal point, coherent backyard depth, integrated mechanical construction, two genuinely different drawings per concept and a readable combined composition.

## Review and interaction acceptance

- Only the Australian Capital Territory landscape and its accessory data and drawings change in the Game feature batch.
- The marker remains at its current rounded coordinates; explanatory copy calls QG2811 representative and preserves the two-stage date evidence.
- Every concept has a dedicated SVG drawing path; none uses the old seedpod maracas, quadrat shield, snorkel or a generic fallback renderer.
- Primary and companion copies differ in outline, construction, internal detail and placement.
- All three pairs remain legible together and do not intersect one another or hide worm faces.
- Each of the six visible objects remains independently draggable and keyboard movable; Home resets only the focused copy.
- Accessible names match the visible objects.
- Pointer and keyboard tests cover all six copies.
- Visual checks cover light and dark themes and responsive widths from 360 px to wide desktop.
- No horizontal overflow, console error, broken source link or change to another location is accepted.
- Environment/accessory audits, syntax checks, `git diff --check` and the full diff pass before integration.
