# Auckland *C. elegans* location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the *Caenorhabditis elegans* Auckland landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the Bristol N2, Santeuil, Edinburgh, Tenerife, Kauaʻi and Australian Capital Territory batches in the location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow.

## Evidence boundaries

### Representative collection record

The current Game marker says `Auckland, New Zealand` at -36.893, 174.746. Current CaeNDR data identify one unambiguous match, **ECA36**, formerly GD6, at coordinates only about 56 m from that rounded marker:

- species: *Caenorhabditis elegans*;
- strain and isotype reference: ECA36;
- previous name: GD6;
- coordinates: -36.893333, 174.745529;
- collection date: 27 July 2013;
- elevation: 46 m;
- landscape: urban garden;
- substrate category: vegetal litter or mix;
- substrate comment: garden grass;
- sampler: I. Ly;
- isolator: K. Ly.

CaeNDR's exact microhabitat stops at `garden grass`. The grass taxon, lawn versus detached litter, clipping state, soil, moisture, associated plant, weather, garden layout and isolation method are not recorded. The precise coordinates appear to identify a private urban property; public artwork must remain anonymised and must not reconstruct, identify or disclose it.

Primary sources:

- [CaeNDR ECA36 isotype page](https://caendr.org/isotype/ECA36/)

### Correct regional setting

The existing harbour, islands, beach, close crater cone and pōhutukawa frame are not supported as the collection setting. ECA36 came from an inland urban garden; a particular viewpoint or visible landmark is not recorded.

Auckland's volcanic field and skyline may appear only as restrained regional orientation. Its maunga are ancestral mountains of profound living significance for Mana Whenua. They remain respectful, subordinate landscape forms and never become hats, toys, extracted scoria or generic Māori decoration.

Regional sources:

- [Tūpuna Maunga Authority, Auckland's ancestral mountains](https://maunga.nz/the-ancestral-mountains-of-auckland)
- [Engineering New Zealand, Auckland Sky Tower heritage record](https://www.engineeringnz.org/programmes/heritage/engineering-heritage-records/buildings/auckland-sky-tower/)

These pages are factual and visual references only. The website uses an original SVG illustration and does not trace an external photograph, live skyline, private property or institutional graphic.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects the documented garden-grass substrate to an anonymised compact urban garden and very restrained regional context. It must not become an Auckland tourism panorama.

### Foreground: exact grass-litter microhabitat

- Two unequal loose patches of unidentified garden grass and mixed vegetal litter are the closest forms.
- One patch folds or tangles over a darker underside; the other has a different loose, flattened construction.
- The ground is neither a manicured lawn, compost pile, fruit substrate nor field quadrat.
- No grass species, clipping process, soil moisture or associated plant is invented.

### Middle ground: anonymised urban garden

- An uneven garden edge, offset pavers, low non-identifying fence or hedge and fragments of ordinary roofs build one compact space.
- No recognisable house, address, exact property plan or repeated row of suburban icons appears.
- Garden materials overlap in depth and lead into the regional background rather than forming horizontal bands.

### Background: Auckland lock silhouette

- A restrained roofline, two separated low volcanic-field shoulders and one hairline Sky Tower cue provide regional orientation only.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is asymmetric grass-litter folds below a broken fence and roofline, two low separated shoulders and one fine city needle.
- There is no harbour, island chain, beach, close crater, pōhutukawa frame or pā-terrace ornament.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate the exact substrate, defensible volcanic-field science and ECA36's published laboratory phenotype into movable teaching objects. They are not claims about collection equipment or local traditional objects.

### ECA36 grass-litter profiler

This fictional inspection mechanism teaches the recorded substrate without pretending that it was used to isolate ECA36.

- **Primary:** a broad low arched transparent chamber on complete rails, with irregular green-brown litter layers, hinged inspection doors, sliding magnifier and small ECA36 ticket.
- **Companion:** a tall zig-zag roller profiler carrying a narrow litter ribbon through three staggered illuminated windows, with a connected crank and lower catch drawer.
- The silhouettes are horizontal arch versus vertical zig-zag tower. Every rail, crank and support connects visibly; no unexplained rods are accepted.

### Auckland volcanic-field monitor

GeoNet's monitoring of the Auckland Volcanic Field supplies a defensible scientific concept without trivialising a maunga.

Source: [GeoNet Auckland Volcanic Field monitoring](https://www.geonet.org.nz/volcano/monitoring/aucklandvolcanicfield).

- **Primary:** a wide paper-drum seismograph in a four-foot frame, with suspended mass, stylus and an original zig-zag trace.
- **Companion:** a tall triangular vibration station with enclosed pendulum, circular amplitude dial, three connected sensor feet and a folded side chart.
- The drawings do not copy GeoNet branding, live data or named-maunga outlines.

### ECA36 reproductive-timing clock

A controlled 96-strain laboratory study found ECA36 an outlier for both brood size and male-production rate. This is a laboratory comparison, not a field observation or proof of local adaptation.

Source: [Lim, Kim and Lee, 2021, Natural variation in reproductive traits of *C. elegans* wild isolates](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8664432/).

- **Primary:** a broad circular 72-hour clock split into 0–36 and 36–72-hour sectors, with an outer progeny-bead rail, small male-tally aperture and ECA36 tag.
- **Companion:** a tall bifurcating flip-counter with separate early and late windows, unequal progeny and male tally chutes and a small X-segregation gate.
- The drawing does not print a percentage unless it can also carry a clear `lab assay` qualifier without clutter.

## Cultural, privacy and scientific safeguards

- Keep the public locality `Auckland, New Zealand`; do not expose or reconstruct the likely private property.
- Do not claim a particular maunga, harbour, skyline or tower was visible from the sample.
- Do not use named Tūpuna Maunga, pā terraces, tihi, scoria, koru, tā moko, carving, waka or other Māori cultural forms as costumes or playful props.
- Do not infer a grass species or collection and isolation apparatus.
- Keep the reproductive result explicitly laboratory-derived.

## Quality benchmark

The revised Bristol N2, Santeuil, Edinburgh, Tenerife, Kauaʻi and ACT batches are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. Auckland must exceed them through an exact grass-litter focal point, coherent anonymised garden depth, integrated mechanisms, two genuinely different drawings per concept and a readable combined composition.

## Review and interaction acceptance

- Only the *C. elegans* Auckland landscape and its accessory data and drawings change in the Game feature batch.
- The public marker remains at its current rounded coordinates and gains the ECA36 representative; explanatory copy separates exact evidence from regional context and laboratory phenotype.
- Every concept has a dedicated SVG drawing path; none uses the old bowler, sail wings, pōhutukawa umbrella or a generic fallback renderer.
- Primary and companion copies differ in outline, construction, internal detail and placement.
- All three pairs remain legible together and do not intersect one another or hide worm faces.
- Each of the six visible objects remains independently draggable and keyboard movable; Home resets only the focused copy.
- Accessible names match the visible objects.
- Pointer and keyboard tests cover all six copies, including after a responsive resize.
- Visual checks cover light and dark themes and responsive widths from 360 px to wide desktop.
- No horizontal overflow, console error, broken source link or change to another location is accepted.
- Environment/accessory audits, syntax checks, `git diff --check` and the full diff pass before integration.
