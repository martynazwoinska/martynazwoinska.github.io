# New Taipei City *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* NIC1648 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
New Taipei City NIC1648 environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative choice and current-marker caveat

Use NIC1648, previously 3b, as the explicit representative.

NIC1648 is a whole-genome-sequenced, distributable CaeNDR isotype-reference
strain with no reported strain issue. Its record provides:

- exact coordinates and elevation;
- a forest landscape;
- an explicit 10–15 mm substrate-size range;
- a precise named district and trail destination;
- the substrate's position on and beside concrete stairs;
- collector, isolator and collection date;
- ITS2 as the species-identification method;
- separate notes that the strain was not bleached and that many males were
  observed.

The current Game coordinate `[121.773, 25.053]` is a rounded match to the
NIC1544–NIC1550 cluster at `[121.77265, 25.05343]`, approximately 59 m away.
Those strains came from figs on or beside a stone path after the fifth bridge
on the Mt Wufen trail. Their evidence must not be combined with NIC1648.

Choosing NIC1648 intentionally moves the marker approximately 5.67 km east to
its own coordinate.

The active profile nevertheless depicts Yehliu Geopark. The Taiwan Tourism
Administration places Yehliu at 25.205290, 121.68992, approximately 18.9 km
from the current Game marker and approximately 20.8 km from NIC1648. No CaeNDR
record examined connects either marker to Yehliu's coast or hoodoo geology.

Public copy must describe NIC1648 as one representative New Taipei isolate,
not as every New Taipei record.

## Why the current implementation is unsupported

The current scene is `new-taipei-yehliu`, titled `Yehliu sandstone cape`. It
depicts or names:

- an exposed sandstone cape;
- surf and open sea;
- hoodoo or mushroom-rock columns;
- coastal erosion and weathering;
- a hoodoo helmet;
- an erosion crinoline;
- a cape fiddle.

These are real features of Yehliu Geopark, but no collection evidence connects
them to NIC1648 or to the rounded NIC1544-cluster marker currently used by the
Game.

The current marker is not at Yehliu, and the strain records describe forest
substrates rather than marine rock platforms. The scene and all three
accessories must therefore be replaced rather than relabelled.

## Exact-versus-context evidence boundary

Exact NIC1648 evidence:

- strain NIC1648 and previous name 3b;
- isotype-reference, WGS and distribution status;
- coordinates and 226 m elevation;
- forest landscape;
- small figs measuring 10–15 mm;
- unconfirmed presumed decay;
- Ruifang District, New Taipei City;
- on and beside concrete stairs descending toward Menghuan Falls;
- sampling by C. Gosse on 17 October 2017;
- isolation by C. Braendle;
- ITS2 identification;
- the separate no-bleach and many-males notes.

Illustrative setting only:

- the exact stair geometry;
- leaf-litter distribution;
- generic, unidentified forest trunks and foliage;
- the direction and depth of the forest corridor;
- the number, outline and colour of the depicted figs.

Do not depict the waterfall. Its name fixes the route destination, but the
record does not establish that it was visible from the collection point.

Do not import evidence from other New Taipei strains. In particular:

- Mt Wufen bridges and stone path belong to NIC1541–NIC1550 records;
- shrines belong to NIC1559, NIC1560 and related route descriptions;
- houses and farms belong to other Pingxi records;
- old-railway gravel belongs to NIC1555 and NIC1556;
- Menghuan temple belongs to NIC1644 and NIC1646;
- Lingjiao Falls stairs and terrace belong to NIC1653–NIC1658.

## Environment illustration contract

Create a dedicated original SVG scene in the existing 600 × 430 viewBox.

### Camera

- Use a close, low view looking obliquely across a descending forest stair.
- Preserve central play space approximately from `x=175–440`, `y=155–345`.
- Include no panoramic horizon, coast or broad landmark view.

### Foreground

- Place small, anonymous fig forms across two explicitly different micro-sites:
  one group on a broad concrete tread and another in adjacent leaf litter.
- Make the number, outer shape, colour and decay treatment visibly
  illustrative.
- Use restrained muted plum, umber and ochre rather than a botanically
  identifying fruit treatment.
- Include one simple scale cue in the scene only if it cannot be mistaken for a
  measured field instrument.
- Do not show an attached fig tree or identify a species.

### Middle

- Build one strong broken-diagonal concrete stair flight.
- Let it turn once around the clear central play space, producing a readable
  zig-zag rather than a straight horizontal band.
- Use broad, unequal tread foreshortening to distinguish it from paths, rivers,
  terraces and the current cape.
- Let leaf litter cross some tread edges without making the stairs look ruined
  or unsafe; their condition was not recorded.
- Include no invented handrail, bridge, shrine, temple, sign or house.

### Background

- Close the scene with a steep forest corridor of unequal, unidentified trunks
  and layered foliage.
- Align one narrow route-shaped light opening with the upper stair turn.
- Keep the opening enclosed and terrestrial.
- Include no water, waterfall curtain, sea, skyline, radar station or mountain
  panorama.

### Silhouette lock

> Small fig forms straddle the edge of one broad concrete tread beneath a
> descending zig-zag stair corridor, framed by unequal forest trunks around one
> narrow route-shaped opening.

This silhouette must be immediately distinguishable from:

- Taipei BRC20390's boulder-and-fern foothill with Taipei 101;
- Yehliu's long coastal platform and mushroom rocks;
- the repeated closed-forest fruit scenes elsewhere in the Game.

### Forbidden

- Yehliu cape, hoodoos, sandstone strata, surf or open sea.
- Waterfall, stream, pool, visible spray or rain.
- Taipei 101 or another skyline.
- Mt Wufen ridge panorama, radar station or numbered bridge.
- Temple, shrine, red pillars or religious ornament.
- Railway, mine, sky lantern or tourism shorthand.
- House, ruined building, farm or private-property detail.
- Lingjiao Falls or evidence from another New Taipei strain.
- Identified fig species, attached fig tree or invented associated organism.
- Confirmed-decay claim or diagnosed fruit disease.
- Invented stair count, dimensions, handrail or construction history.
- Male worms presented as a field association with the figs.
- National symbols, costume motifs or decorative pseudo-traditional patterns.

All six drawings are movable scientific or provenance mechanisms, not
costumes.

### NIC1648 10–15 mm fig comparator

Family ID: `nic1648-fig-diameter-comparator`

Exact basis: the strain record describes small figs with a diameter range of
10–15 mm.

Primary: a broad horizontal twin-jaw comparator bench with separate fixed
`10 mm` and `15 mm` stops, two unequal blank specimen cups, an exposed
micrometer drum, linked pointer and separate NIC1648 culture vial.

Companion: a tall asymmetric ring-gauge tower with two visibly different
apertures marked `10` and `15`, a counterweighted sliding sample cup, exposed
chain, lower receiving drawer and offset NIC1648 plate.

The drawings encode the recorded range; they are not reconstructions of a
field instrument. Do not imply a mean, exact specimen measurement, sample
count or fig species.

### NIC1648 stair-side sample indexer

Family ID: `nic1648-stair-side-sample-indexer`

Exact basis: the substrate was recorded on the concrete stairs and beside
them.

Primary: a wide low plan-view indexing deck with two offset tread slabs, one
`ON STEP` specimen shutter, one `BESIDE` litter-pocket shutter, a visibly
connected coordinate drum and a separate vial cradle.

Companion: a tall folding stair column with alternating tread plates, one
offset side-litter drawer, a descending chain linking both collection zones,
a side crank and reversible `NIC1648 / 3b` plate.

The primary must read horizontally and the companion vertically. Do not encode
an exact stair count, landing arrangement, distance, route map or waterfall
view.

### NIC1648 qualitative male viewer

Family ID: `nic1648-qualitative-male-viewer`

Exact basis: the strain record qualitatively notes many males but gives no
number, proportion, observation method, stage or cause.

Primary: a broad multi-window observation drum with abstract worm-form strips,
one male-tail reference gate, a movable `MALES NOTED` shutter, exposed drive
gears and separate NIC1648 vial. Include no numbered tally.

Companion: a tall two-channel optical column with staggered sighting apertures,
a rotating field strip, one qualitative dial marked `NOTED`, an offset focus
wheel and lower culture drawer.

Do not imply:

- a male count, percentage or sex ratio;
- that the observation occurred in the original field sample;
- a heritable phenotype or population-wide property;
- a connection to Medea, mating success or gene drive;
- a causal relationship with the separate no-bleach note;
- an experimental instrument or assay not recorded by the source.
