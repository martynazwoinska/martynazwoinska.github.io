# Tenerife location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the Tenerife landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the Bristol N2, Santeuil and Edinburgh batches in the location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow.

## Evidence boundaries

### Collection record set and representative

The Game marker says `Tenerife, Spain` at 28.411, -16.535. Current CaeNDR data place 23 *Caenorhabditis elegans* strains, NIC1780 through NIC1802, at the more precise coordinates 28.411121, -16.535468. All share:

- collection date: 14 March 2019;
- site: Botanical garden, Puerto de la Cruz;
- landscape: botanical garden or zoo;
- sampler: C. Braendle;
- isolators: C. Gimond, C. Ferrari and C. Braendle.

The records cover several substrates: twelve rotting nuts, pods, seeds or fruits; five rotting flowers; four rotting stems; and two vegetal-litter mixes. The marker must therefore not be presented as one uniquely identified isolate or substrate.

This illustration uses **NIC1787** as an explicit representative because it is a distributable isotype/reference strain without a recorded issue and has unusually precise substrate metadata:

- species: *Caenorhabditis elegans*;
- strain and isotype reference: NIC1787;
- coordinates: 28.411121, -16.535468;
- elevation: 114 m;
- landscape: botanical garden or zoo;
- substrate category: rotting nut, pod, seed or fruit;
- substrate comment: `Avocado fruit, old, very rotten`;
- previous name: 9.1.

The old, very rotten avocado is exact record evidence. Its position on the garden floor is a restrained illustration inference; neither CaeNDR nor the artwork should claim that the record states whether the fruit was fallen, attached or collected beside a particular tree.

### Correct local setting

The CaeNDR coordinates identify the Jardín de Aclimatación de La Orotava in Puerto de la Cruz. Despite its historical name, the garden is in Puerto de la Cruz rather than the municipality of La Orotava. The existing high-caldera scene therefore mislocates the collection and is retired.

The regional research institute's visitor page prints a longitude that is incompatible with both Puerto de la Cruz and the CaeNDR record. The artwork retains the current CaeNDR coordinates and does not reproduce that apparent typographical error. Its roughly 120 m garden altitude is consistent with CaeNDR's 114 m sample elevation.

Documented wider garden context includes:

- a historic walled plan of 24 rectangular Linnaean classes;
- paths, ponds, tropical and subtropical collections, a herbarium, laboratories and a germplasm bank;
- deliberate retention of dry leaves and plant litter as part of the garden's natural cycle;
- an irrigation reservoir dating to 1795;
- a prominent *Ficus macrophylla* f. *columnaris* with aerial-root columns.

These are setting references, not claims about NIC1787's exact sampling point. The Orotava Valley slopes, sea and Teide are regional context only. A tiny muted Teide notch may appear through the canopy, but Teide National Park, Las Cañadas, lava tongues and a dominant sea-of-clouds volcano must not be presented as the collection habitat.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects NIC1787's exact avocado microhabitat to a coherent historic botanical-garden space. It must not become a row of tropical plant symbols or a disguised Teide panorama.

### Foreground: recorded microhabitat

- One unmistakably old, collapsed avocado is the closest object.
- Its pear-shaped dark rind, browned softened flesh and exposed pit distinguish it from a pristine fruit and from N2's circular agar plates.
- Damp leaf litter, decomposing plant matter and small irregular garden-floor details surround it without obscuring the fruit.
- Ground placement is illustrative and must not be described as an exact recorded position.

### Middle ground: historic garden space

- Unequal rectangular beds and orthogonal paths converge through one continuous view.
- The old irrigation pond or reservoir provides a restrained water focus rather than a generic tropical lagoon.
- Botanical labels, water plants and varied vegetation may add depth without forming a repeated icon strip.
- A large asymmetric *Ficus* aerial-root colonnade frames one side. It is wider garden context, not the claimed sample tree.

### Background: Puerto de la Cruz lock silhouette

- A garden wall and green Orotava slope close the scene.
- A very small muted Teide notch may appear in one canopy opening as regional context only.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is a split pear-shaped avocado beneath an asymmetric root arch, crossing paths around one pond and a tiny distant peak.
- There is no Las Cañadas rim, dominant cone, lava field, Teide-broom foreground or ruler-flat sea of clouds.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate the representative substrate and documented garden into movable educational objects. They are original playful mechanisms, not claims about traditional Tenerife clothing or historical instruments.

### Avocado microhabitat viewer

- **Primary:** a low horizontal pear-shaped cutaway with mottled rind, collapsed browned flesh, central pit and hinged antique-brass magnifying hood.
- **Companion:** a tall folding peel triptych whose panels open like doors, with an off-centre pit-viewing lens and a separate shallow litter drawer.
- The drawings use no circular Petri-dish outline, unexplained rods or brown sticks and cannot be mistaken for N2's agar plate.

### Aerial-root harp

The form is a playful translation of the garden's documented *Ficus* rather than an authentic local instrument.

- **Primary:** a tall asymmetric living-branch arch; multiple unequal thickening root columns become strings; a broad dark-green leaf is the resonator; small fig-shaped pegs tune it.
- **Companion:** a low trapezoidal buttress-root frame with only three chunky root strings, an offset branch soundbox and different foot and anchor geometry.
- Under the conservative semantic ledger, this is the second and final plucked-string family after the Kauaʻi QG130 waterfall lyre. Its outline, material and construction must remain radically different.

### Linnaean seed-exchange engine

This invented teaching model is grounded in the documented 24-class plan, herbarium and international germplasm exchange. It is not claimed to be a historical garden instrument.

- **Primary:** a wide wood-and-brass six-by-four accession board with a rotating bridge of seed capsules and one leaf-shaped index pointer.
- **Companion:** a tall folding cabinet with three banks of eight differently sized drawers, a central herbarium-sheet panel and a side crank.
- Neither drawing may reuse Edinburgh's portal scanner, a generic backpack or a plain labelled rectangle.

## Quality benchmark

The revised Bristol N2, Santeuil and Edinburgh batches are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. Tenerife must exceed them through an exact record-led foreground, coherent garden depth, botanical construction detail, two genuinely different mechanisms per concept and a readable combined composition.

## Review and interaction acceptance

- Only the Tenerife landscape and Tenerife accessory data and drawings change in the Game feature batch.
- The public marker remains `Tenerife, Spain`, while explanatory copy calls NIC1787 representative and does not collapse the 23-record set into one strain.
- Every concept has a dedicated SVG drawing path; none uses the old goggles, broom plume, glider or a generic fallback renderer.
- Primary and companion copies differ in outline, construction, internal detail and placement.
- All three pairs remain legible together and do not hide worm faces.
- Each of the six visible objects remains independently draggable and keyboard movable; Home resets only the focused copy.
- Accessible names match the visible objects.
- Pointer and keyboard tests cover all six copies.
- Visual checks cover light and dark themes and responsive widths from 360 px to wide desktop.
- No horizontal overflow, console error, broken source link or change to another location is accepted.
- Environment/accessory audits, syntax checks, `git diff --check` and the full diff pass before integration.
