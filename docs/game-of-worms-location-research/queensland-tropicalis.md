# Queensland *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* QG2904 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Queensland QG2904 environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative choice

Use QG2904 from sample 179uc as the explicit representative.

QG2904 is:

- an isotype-reference strain;
- whole-genome sequenced;
- distributable through CaeNDR;
- recorded without a strain issue;
- supported by exact coordinates and elevation;
- tied to the Daintree Rainforest Observatory;
- tied to the explicit substrate description “uncracked blackbean pod”;
- supported by collection, isolation, funnel-plating and line-founding dates;
- identified by mating.

The current CaeNDR release contains three relevant sample pairs at the Daintree
Rainforest Observatory. Their evidence must remain separate.

were biologically absent from the source population.

QG2904 and QG2905 are separate isotype-reference lines from the same sample.
Public copy must describe QG2904 as one representative line, not as the whole
sample or every Queensland isolate.

## Substrate-identity boundary

Queensland Government sources use “black bean” for *Castanospermum australe*
and document large, heavy, rounded pods. That makes the species a plausible
common-name reference.

However, the QG2904 record supplies no scientific plant name, voucher,
photograph, pod measurements, seed count or confirmation of the source tree.
Public strain copy must retain “uncracked blackbean pod”, not silently upgrade
the record to *Castanospermum australe*.

The official Queensland plant information may guide a generic heavy,
bean-like, sealed-pod silhouette as regional botanical context. The artwork
must not label the pod with a scientific species name.

The structured CaeNDR substrate category includes rotting, but the specific
comment and notes say only “uncracked blackbean”. Do not depict visible rot or
claim that the pod was decomposing.

## Why the current implementation is unsupported

The active scene is “queensland-daintree”, titled “Daintree forest-to-reef
coast”. It depicts or names a white-sand beach, open coast, reef, mangrove, fan
palm, bright sun and an uninterrupted forest-to-sand-to-reef transect.

The current accessories are:

- fan-palm collar;
- reef monocle;
- continuum skates.

The Daintree region genuinely contains rainforest, beaches, mangroves and
nearby reef systems. James Cook University says these other ecosystems are
accessible within a few kilometres of the observatory.

That regional proximity does not make them the QG2904 collection patch.
QG2904 is recorded in forest at 76 m at the observatory, from an uncracked
blackbean pod. The source does not connect the sample to a beach, reef,
mangrove, fan palm or coastal sightline.

The beach-and-reef scene and all three accessories must be replaced.
