# Trivandrum *C. nigoni* location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the *Caenorhabditis nigoni* Trivandrum landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the evidence-led, location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow established for the revised *C. elegans* records.

## Evidence boundaries

### Type-isolate collection record

The Game already labels `Trivandrum, Kerala · JU1325`, but its rounded marker and backwater concept do not describe the published record. **JU1325** is the *C. nigoni* type isolate, originally known as *Caenorhabditis* sp. 9:

- species: *Caenorhabditis nigoni*;
- strain and type isolate: JU1325;
- coordinates: 8.512, 76.955;
- collection date: 21 December 2007;
- site: Zoo/Botanical Garden of Trivandrum, now officially Thiruvananthapuram;
- substrate: rotting flowers and leaves;
- collector: M.-A. Félix;
- recovery: the sample remained in a plastic tube for two weeks before nematodes were isolated on OP50-seeded agar.

No collector-recorded elevation was found. A terrain-model value is not strain metadata and must not be printed as though it were recorded at collection. The flower and leaf species, exact plant, exact garden position and whether the material was fallen are unknown.

### Correct garden setting

The existing backwater, sandbar, mangrove, coconut strip and stepped Western Ghats are broad regional shorthand rather than collection evidence and are retired.

The official garden description documents a hill-and-dale site with paths, terraces, lawns, lakes, shrubberies, flowerbeds and conservatories rising from a lower lake to the top garden. It identifies golden bamboo and the museum above the garden. The official zoo page confirms a wooded natural lake; the master plan documents a bandstand and lake-fed gardening infrastructure; the museum page identifies the Napier Museum's Gothic roof and minarets.

These are wider campus context, not the recorded sampling point. The artwork must not claim that JU1325 was collected beside the lake, bamboo, bandstand or museum.

Authoritative setting sources:

## Landscape composition contract

The scene connects the exact flower-and-leaf substrate to one continuous sloping botanical-garden view. It must not become a row of tropical symbols, zoo animals or a generic Kerala panorama.

### Foreground: exact decay microhabitat

- One off-centre, visibly collapsed mixture of anonymous wilted flowers and decomposing leaves is the closest focal form.
- Layered petals, curled leaf edges, damp dark material and restrained decay cues distinguish the substrate without assigning plant identity.
- The patch is irregular and ground-like rather than another circular culture dish.
- No sacred, culturally specific or named flower is inferred.

### Middle ground: garden slope

- A curved wooded-lake edge and one rising path split across unequal terraces.
- Restrained lawns and flowerbeds follow the slope rather than forming repeated horizontal bands.
- One asymmetric golden-bamboo mass gives the garden a documented botanical landmark without claiming it as the sample plant.
- Water, paths, beds and vegetation remain one spatially connected garden.

### Background: Trivandrum lock silhouette

- Top-garden canopy partly screens a simplified original silhouette of the Napier Museum's Gothic roof and unequal minarets.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is a collapsed flower-and-leaf bundle below a curved lake, with tiered paths climbing between a bamboo fan and partly hidden museum roof.
- There is no backwater, sandbar, mangrove, houseboat, Western Ghats panorama or generic coconut icon strip.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories translate JU1325's documented recovery history and wider garden infrastructure into movable teaching objects. They are original mechanisms, not claimed historic artefacts or traditional Kerala objects.

### JU1325 sample-tube timekeeper

- **Primary:** a broad horizontal transparent tube in a brass rotating yoke; separate petal and leaf layers, stopper, date tag, fourteen-segment time ring and offset hinged transfer chute ending at a small culture deck.
- **Companion:** a tall upright twin-chamber tube with petals above curled leaves, side sampling port, vertical day counter, crooked tripod and slide-out agar drawer at the base.
- The drawings do not become two resized cylinders and cannot resemble N2's cryovial jetpacks or agar-plate objects.

### Trivandrum garden waterworks

This playful teaching model is grounded in documented lakes, rising terraces and lake-fed garden watering; it is not claimed historical equipment.

- **Primary:** a wide low lake basin with an exposed handwheel pump feeding three unequal zig-zag terrace channels and small planted beds.
- **Companion:** a tall narrow pump column beside two stacked cisterns, an S-shaped delivery pipe, offset leaf sprinklers and separate sight gauge.
- One construction is horizontal and openly mechanical; the other is vertical and hydraulic.

### Trivandrum bandstand music box

The garden's documented bandstand provides a local musical mechanism without imitating a named traditional instrument.

- **Primary:** a broad octagonal pavilion with eight slender columns, shallow faceted roof, visible horizontal pinned music cylinder, winding crank and resonator slats.
- **Companion:** a tall folding three-arch cabinet with a steeper canopy, vertical perforated music roll, two hanging sound reeds and triangular base.
- The design is an original mechanical translation, not a reconstruction or celebration of the bandstand's former military use.

## Cultural and ecological safeguards

- Do not use Kathakali costume, temple imagery, elephants, sari costume, houseboats or national-colour shorthand.
- Do not portray anonymous sample flowers as sacred or culturally specific species.
- Do not imply collecting present-day garden material or disturbing protected specimens.
- Keep `Trivandrum` in the marker because it is the historical collection wording; use `Thiruvananthapuram` for the present institution where appropriate.

## Quality benchmark

The revised preceding locations are the minimum quality floor, not templates to repeat. Trivandrum must exceed them through an exact decay focal point, coherent sloping garden depth, integrated mechanisms, two genuinely different drawings per concept and a readable combined composition.

## Review and interaction acceptance

- Only the Trivandrum landscape and its accessory data and drawings change in the Game feature batch.
- The marker moves to the published JU1325 coordinates and explanatory copy distinguishes the exact record from wider garden context.
- Every concept has a dedicated SVG drawing path; none uses the old coconut hood, backwater skates, sandbar drum or a generic fallback renderer.
- Primary and companion copies differ in outline, construction, internal detail and placement.
- All three pairs remain legible together and do not intersect one another or hide worm faces.
- Each of the six visible objects remains independently draggable and keyboard movable; Home resets only the focused copy.
- Accessible names match the visible objects.
- Pointer and keyboard tests cover all six copies, including after a responsive resize.
- Visual checks cover light and dark themes and responsive widths from 360 px to wide desktop.
- No horizontal overflow, console error, broken source link or change to another location is accepted.
- Environment/accessory audits, syntax checks, `git diff --check` and the full diff pass before integration.
