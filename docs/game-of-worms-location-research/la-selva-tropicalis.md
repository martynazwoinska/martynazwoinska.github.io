# La Selva *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* QG3845 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
La Selva, Costa Rica environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative collection record

Use QG3845 as the explicit representative:

- Species: *Caenorhabditis tropicalis*
- Strain and isotype reference: QG3845
- Other strain in the isotype: QG3844
- Coordinates: 10.43082, -84.01138
- Elevation: 65 m
- Landscape: forest
- Substrate category: rotting nut, pod, seed or fruit
- Substrate comment: `brown lemon-looking thing`
- Substrate temperature: 26.5 °C
- Collection date: 2 July 2019
- Sampled by: A. Martel
- Isolated by: A. Martel
- Species-identification method: mating
- Sample note: AM10-72.1

“Brown lemon-looking thing” is a field description, not an identification.
The fruit remains anonymous. Its species, attachment or ground placement,
dimensions, internal structure, associated organism and exact decay geometry
were not recorded.

CaeNDR records ambient temperature and humidity as missing. Weather, nearby
plant identities, canopy structure, exact trail, distance to water and a
collection-site photograph are also unreported.

## Survey and culture context

Sloat et al. collected nematodes at La Selva in July 2019 using Baermann
funnels. They used ITS2 sequencing and established cultures followed by
species tests. Established cultures remained on NGM plates at La Selva for
about six months before transport to New York.

The supporting dataset identifies AM10 as a fruit sample and records QG3845,
QG3846, QG3848 and an FTA specimen from that patch. QG3845 is representative,
not the only organism recovered from the sample.

CaeNDR records QG3845 as returned to New York on 24 December 2019 and later
processed on a 10 cm plate. Those laboratory dates must not be presented as
collection dates.

CaeNDR’s free-text note says use is governed by Costa Rican permits, and a
2026 genomic study states that Costa Rican strains are excluded from
availability because of restrictive permits. Public Game copy links to the
record but makes no availability or strain-request claim.

## Exact-versus-regional evidence boundary

Exact evidence:

- QG3845 strain and isotype-reference status
- QG3844 as the other strain in the isotype
- Coordinates, 65 m elevation and forest landscape
- Rotting anonymous fruit and the field description
- Substrate temperature of 26.5 °C
- Sampling and isolation by A. Martel on 2 July 2019
- Species identification by mating
- Baermann-funnel recovery at survey level
- Six-month plate residence at La Selva at study level

Regional context only:

- La Selva’s wet lowland tropical forest
- Old-growth and recovering forest within the reserve
- The trail network and the forested Barva elevational corridor
- Rivers bordering La Selva
- Barva’s 2,906 m summit
- Leaf-cutter ants observed and researched at La Selva
- Research-station buildings, bridges and canopy facilities

No source shows that QG3845 was collected beside a river, bridge, building,
trail, canopy tower or visible volcano. Leaf-cutter ants are regional forest
context and must not be shown as associated with QG3845.

Camera:

- Use a close, low forest-floor viewpoint.
- Make the exact anonymous fruit patch the focal event.

## Accessory contract

### QG3845 26.5 °C substrate dial

Family ID: `qg3845-substrate-temperature`

Exact basis: CaeNDR records one substrate-temperature value, 26.5 °C. The
instrument used was not recorded, so these objects display the datum rather
than reconstruct collection equipment.

Primary: a broad low semicircular brass-and-glass dial fixed at 26.5 °C, with
one asymmetric anonymous-fruit contact cradle, radial tick ring, mechanical
pointer pivot and reversible `AM10 / QG3845` plate.

Companion: a tall narrow protected column with a sliding 26.5 °C marker, open
leaf-vein-like cage, separate lower contact shoe, small offset verification
dial and differently placed `QG3845` tab.

Show no invented humidity, ambient temperature, time series or instrument
brand.

### La Selva leaf-cutter automaton

Family ID: `la-selva-leafcutter-automaton`

Regional basis: leaf-cutter ants are observed and researched at La Selva. They
are not recorded as associated organisms for QG3845.

Primary: a long low articulated procession on an elliptical cam track, with
four differently posed brass ants carrying three unequal abstract leaf panels,
an exposed crankshaft and complete connecting rods.

Companion: a tall open spiral lift with two ant figures climbing separate
steps, one suspended leaf counterweight, vertical escapement and compact
tripod base.

Do not identify an ant species, claim a colony size or connect ants to the
nematode sample.

### La Selva–Barva gradient lift

Family ID: `barva-gradient-lift`

Regional basis: OTS describes a forested elevational corridor connecting La
Selva with Barva Volcano. QG3845’s exact elevation is 65 m; Barva’s cited
summit is 2,906 m.

Primary: a broad diagonal rack with endpoint tabs `65 m` and `2,906 m`, one
continuous forest ribbon, sliding leaf carriage, handwheel and restrained mist
screen near the upper endpoint.

Companion: a tall counterweighted lift with separate lowland and summit
windows, braided vertical forest strip, cloud wheel, offset leaf cage and
independent winding drum.

Do not add unsupported vegetation zones, temperatures, shelters, route
geometry or a literal map.
