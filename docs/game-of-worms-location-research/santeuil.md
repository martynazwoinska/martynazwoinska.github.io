# Santeuil location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

This dossier is the evidence and review contract for the Santeuil landscape and its three accessory pairs. It follows the Bristol N2 batch in the location-by-location Game of Worms illustration workflow.

## Evidence boundaries

### Representative collection record

The current Game marker says only `Santeuil, France` at 49.121, 1.951. Santeuil has many recorded *Caenorhabditis elegans* samples and more than one substrate, so the marker must not be described as if it were a single uniquely identified isolate.

This illustration uses **JU1925** as an explicit representative because the official current CaeNDR strain data place it at the Game marker's coordinates and record:

- species: *Caenorhabditis elegans*;
- strain: JU1925;
- coordinates: 49.121, 1.951;
- landscape: forest;
- substrate: rotting common-hogweed, *Heracleum sphondylium*, stem S73;
- sampler and isolator: M.-A. Félix.

JU1925 is a strain in the JU2600 isotype group, not that group's isotype reference. The public location label remains `Santeuil, France`; the artwork and explanatory copy call JU1925 a representative microhabitat rather than the only Santeuil record.

Primary catalogue source: [CaeNDR current *C. elegans* strain data](https://caendr.org/request-strains/download/c_elegans/latest/strain-data/csv).

### Natural microhabitat

Félix and Duveau sampled Santeuil wood over three Octobers. *C. elegans* occurred on about half of 88 rotten stems, while soil yielded it only occasionally. Their study names thick rotting stems including *Heracleum* and *Symphytum* and warns against reverting to the old assumption that soil is the main habitat.

The Santeuil foreground must therefore make a broken, hollow, decomposing hogweed stem the focal substrate. Wet leaf litter, moss and soil may surround it, but they must not replace it.

Source: [Félix and Duveau, 2012, Population dynamics and habitat sharing of natural populations of *C. elegans* and *C. briggsae*](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/1741-7007-10-59), especially the natural-habitat results and Santeuil-wood sampling paragraphs.

A review of natural *Caenorhabditis* ecology describes the Santeuil habitat as decomposing hogweed and comfrey stems on moist banks of a small wooded stream near the railway, below a village dominated by its church. This supports the scene's spatial connection between stem, stream, railway and church; it does not make the church or railway the collection substrate.

Source: [Cutter, Morran and Phillips, 2019, Males, Outcrossing, and Sexual Selection in *Caenorhabditis* Nematodes](https://academic.oup.com/genetics/article/213/1/27/5930552).

### Regional landscape and landmark context

- The Parc naturel régional du Vexin français describes a broad limestone plateau mantled by loess and cut by small valleys. Its Viosne valley is long and narrow, with wet valley-floor habitats and wooded slopes.
- The official Santeuil page describes the rural village, Saint-Pierre-Saint-Paul church and an isolated pond at the bottom of the Couleuvre valley with dragonflies and birds.
- The French Ministry of Culture records the church's Romanesque and Gothic construction phases and its protected status.
- The Ministry also records an unusual cylinder organ in the church: a blonde-walnut secretary-form instrument by François-Goëry Dumont, dated approximately 1815–1819, with wedge-shaped bellows, a 26-key range and sixteen wooden cylinders. Its metal pipes are no longer present.

## Landscape composition contract

The scene is one continuous stream-bank view, not a row of interchangeable field, reed, village and sun symbols.

### Foreground: recorded microhabitat

- A large broken common-hogweed stem lies or leans across the damp bank, with a visible hollow cavity, segmented wall and decomposing interior.
- Comfrey leaves, moss, fungi and irregular leaf litter establish a shaded woodland floor without disguising the stem.
- The narrow stream begins close to the viewer and remains shallow enough to read as a wooded tributary rather than a broad river.

### Middle ground: wooded stream and railway edge

- The stream bends between unequal wooded banks and disappears into the valley.
- A restrained railway line, sleeper rhythm or small bridge cue sits beside or crosses the stream without becoming the main subject.
- Layered trunks, understorey and wet-bank plants build depth; repeated generic tree icons are not sufficient.

### Background: Santeuil lock silhouette

- A compact village climbs one valley side.
- Saint-Pierre-Saint-Paul rises above it with a central tower and narrow stone spire; the silhouette is architectural context, not a claim that worms were collected at the church.
- A ruler-flat open Vexin plateau appears beyond the valley. There are no mountains, tropical ridges or a broad marsh horizon.
- The locked thumbnail silhouette is a narrow stream-bank notch, one church tower on the slope and the flat plateau above.

## Accessory evidence and design contract

The accessories are playful translations of the representative substrate and documented local objects. They are not claims about traditional Santeuil clothing.

### Hollow-stem specimen lantern

This object teaches the JU1925 substrate by making the hollow, segmented plant stem visible rather than hiding it behind a generic plant icon.

- **Primary:** tall segmented hogweed-stalk frame; cutaway hollow nodes; warm glass specimen chamber; restrained rot, fungus and worm detail; radial umbel finial; coherent handle and base.
- **Companion:** low horizontal lantern assembled from diagonal stem sections; different hinge and handle; separate seed-cluster cage; no recoloured or merely scaled copy of the primary.
- The stem is common hogweed, not giant hogweed, bamboo or a green laboratory tube.

### Santeuil cylinder organ

The real local organ supplies unusually specific construction details and a musical object unlike the Game's existing flutes, fiddles and accordions.

- **Primary:** blonde-walnut secretary-form cabinet; visible pinned cylinder; wedge bellows; side crank; restrained suggestion of the 26-key range; an open or absent pipe rack that honestly reflects the instrument's present missing metal pipework.
- **Companion:** compact vertical organette with a different tower-like cabinet, separate cylinder window, offset crank and another bellows geometry.
- The drawings are original abstractions based on documented construction, not copies of a heritage photograph.

### Couleuvre dragonfly automaton

The official Santeuil description specifically identifies dragonflies at the isolated Couleuvre-valley pond. The accessory is a kinetic observation toy, not a wearable third reuse of the Game's wing family.

- **Primary:** hand-cranked pond-ring mechanism; a long-bodied damselfly-like figure with narrow paired wings; visible linkage, gear or cam; stable base.
- **Companion:** asymmetric carousel with a broader four-wing dragonfly figure, offset crank, different body joints and a distinct pond or reed base.
- The mechanisms remain readable when all three accessory pairs are visible together.

## Quality benchmark

The revised Bristol N2 artwork is the minimum quality floor, not a template to repeat. Santeuil must exceed it through site-specific environmental depth, carefully constructed materials, two genuinely different drawings per concept and a coherent combined composition.

## Review and interaction acceptance

- Only the Santeuil landscape and Santeuil accessory data/drawings change in the Game feature batch.
- Every concept has a dedicated drawing path; none silently falls back to a generic accessory.
- Primary and companion copies differ in outline, construction, internal detail and placement.
- All three pairs remain legible together and do not hide worm faces.
- Each of the six visible objects remains directly draggable and keyboard movable.
- Moving or resetting one copy never moves its partner.
- Accessible names match the visible objects.
- Pointer and keyboard tests cover all six copies.
- Visual checks cover light and dark themes and responsive widths from 360 px to wide desktop.
- No horizontal overflow, console error, broken source link or change to another location is accepted.
- Environment/accessory audits, syntax checks, `git diff --check` and the full diff pass before integration.
