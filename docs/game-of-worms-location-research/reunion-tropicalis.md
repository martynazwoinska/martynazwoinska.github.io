# Saint-Benoît, Réunion *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* JU1373 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-16

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Réunion *C. tropicalis* environment and its three paired accessories. It
replaces the generic cirque, lava and piton treatment with a representative
collection record and a location-specific agricultural microhabitat.

## Representative choice

Use **JU1373** as the explicit representative.

JU1373 is:

- the type isolate designated in the 2014 description of *C. tropicalis*;
- the CaeNDR isotype-reference strain;
- previously called R4D1;
- whole-genome sequenced and distributable through CaeNDR;
- supported by exact coordinates and elevation;
- tied to agricultural land and rotting torch-ginger flowers;
- tied to named sampling and isolation records;
- recorded without a strain issue.

The 2014 species description identifies Saint-Benoît, La Réunion as the type
isolate locality. CaeNDR supplies the coordinate, elevation and substrate
record. Public copy may therefore say that JU1373 is the type isolate and the
CaeNDR isotype-reference strain, but it must not imply that every Réunion
isolate belongs to this one sample.

Réunion. Their evidence must remain separate.

JU1800 is a strong dated alternative, but it is not the designated type

## Date boundary

CaeNDR's structured field gives 31 December 2007, while the free-text sample
comment says early January 2008. This may reflect data normalisation, but the
source does not explain the discrepancy.

Public copy must preserve both records or use the cautious wording
"around the turn of 2007–2008." It must not present either date as an
uncontested exact collection day.

The artwork must not turn the date discrepancy into a claimed field timeline.

## Botanical-name boundary

The CaeNDR comment spells the plant as `Etlingeria elatior`. Kew accepts
*Etlingera elatior*. The corrected accepted spelling may be used in public
copy, with the CaeNDR wording retained in the research record if necessary.

Kew describes the species as a rhizomatous geophyte of the wet tropical biome.
Missouri Botanical Garden documents large leaves on tall pseudostems and
separate flower stalks bearing cone-like inflorescences with overlapping
bracts. Those sources may constrain a generic torch-ginger silhouette.

They do not establish:

- that the illustrated plant was the exact source individual;
- flower colour, number, size or decay state in the JU1373 sample;
- whether the sample was attached or on the ground;
- that torch ginger is native to Réunion;
- the arrangement of plants at the farm.

Do not label the illustration as an exact botanical reconstruction or claim
native status.

## Population-note boundary

The CaeNDR free-text record qualitatively says "Highly proliferating
population." It provides no count, percentage, observation duration,
generation time, growth curve or comparison group.

An accessory may visualise a qualitative observation through expanding
windows or an unnumbered record shutter. It must not display a numerical rate,
rank, time scale, population count or causal explanation.

The free-text hermaphrodite observation describes the sample record. It does
not establish a sex ratio or the absence of males.

## Species-diagnosis context

The 2014 species description states that JU1373 is the type isolate of
*C. tropicalis*, previously *Caenorhabditis* sp. 11. The diagnosis used
fertile crosses in both directions and reports self-fertile hermaphrodites and
facultative males. It also distinguishes the species through SSU, LSU and ITS2
sequence evidence.

This supports a restrained type-isolate accessory and educational copy. It
does not support recreating a specific historical apparatus, cross layout,
laboratory result table or DNA sequence in the artwork.

## Exact-versus-context evidence boundary

Exact JU1373 evidence:

- strain and type-isolate identity;
- R4D1 and *Caenorhabditis* sp. 11 name history;
- coordinate and 129 m elevation;
- Saint-Benoît, Réunion locality;
- agricultural-land classification;
- rotting torch-ginger flower substrate;
- V. Robert in the structured sampler field; Valérie Robert and Loïc Sablé in
  the free-text collection note and CGC record; M.-A. Félix as isolator;
- structured 31 December 2007 date and conflicting early-January-2008 note;
- qualitative hermaphrodite and highly-proliferating-population notes;
- isotype-reference, WGS and distribution status.

Authoritative regional context:

- Saint-Benoît lies on Réunion's humid, windward eastern side;
- the east-facing route rises from cultivated lowland through greener upland
  vegetation;
- volcanic uplands shape the wider horizon;
- cultivated lowland gives way to greener volcanic uplands along the eastern
  route.

Illustrative setting only:

- exact flower outline, colour, decay and ground placement;
- number, height and position of torch-ginger stems and leaves;
- litter pattern and field berm geometry;
- neighbouring agricultural-edge vegetation, treated as unidentified and
  illustrative;
- view direction, cloud cover and visibility of an upland shoulder;
- presence and geometry of a narrow ravine-like notch in the regional view.

The scene must clearly distinguish exact microhabitat evidence from regional
geography. A small background slope cue must not be described as the sampled
farm's documented view.

## Unknowns

The sources do not report:

- exact farm address, property boundary or field layout;
- the exact collection patch within agricultural land;
- neighbouring crop identity;
- whether cane was visible;
- whether the flower was attached, fallen or carried;
- flower count, dimensions, colour, maturity or precise decay geometry;

- collection method or apparatus;
- line founder or isolation protocol;
- exact worm count, proliferation rate or observation interval;
- male presence or population sex ratio;
- weather, light, rainfall, temperature or humidity;
- whether a ravine, coast, mountain, piton or building was visible;
- a collection photograph.

## Why the current implementation is unsupported

The active profile is `reunion-cirque-volcano`, titled "Réunion's pitons,
cirques, and lava." It presents a cirque interior, a dark lava tongue, a ravine
cut and three pitons.

The current accessories are:

- cirque hoop skirt;
- lava crampons;
- ravine trumpet.

These are generic island-wide volcanic references. They do not represent
JU1373's recorded agricultural land, rotting torch-ginger flower substrate,
type-isolate significance or qualitative field annotation. They also treat
the landscape as a cirque viewpoint that the collection record never reports.

Replace the profile, custom scene, marker and all three accessory pairs.

## Environment illustration contract

Create a dedicated original SVG scene in the existing 600 × 430 viewBox.

### Camera

- Use a close, low agricultural-floor view.
- Look obliquely past the flower substrate toward a narrow background slit.
- Preserve central play space approximately from x=170–435, y=145–345.
- Keep the setting intimate rather than panoramic.

### Foreground

- Place one softened, collapsed torch-ginger inflorescence low in dark farm
  litter.
- Build it from unequal side-profile bracts rather than a perfect radial
  rosette.
- Use restrained berry, oxblood and dusty coral accents within the existing
  site palette.
- Make quantity, colour, precise form, decay and placement visibly
  illustrative.
- Do not show worms, fungus, millipede, mollusk or another associated organism.

### Middle

- Use two unequal tall ginger-leaf clusters on different axes.
- Include separate bare flower-stalk silhouettes to reflect generic plant
  architecture without claiming a source individual.
- Let one enormous diagonal leaf cut across an upper corner and form the main
  silhouette lock.
- Add a shallow crescent field berm and irregular leaf litter.
- Keep the centre open for both worms and all six draggable objects.
- Do not use dominant cane diagonals or JU1375's sawtooth ridge composition.

### Background

- Close most of the horizon with humid, unidentified agricultural-edge
  vegetation silhouettes. Treat this vegetation as illustrative rather than a
  documented crop or farm map.
- Leave one narrow upper-right opening.
- Through it, show only a rounded, cloud-softened green upland shoulder and a
  restrained V-shaped vegetation notch as regional context.
- Keep the regional view subordinate to the flower and leaves.
- Do not show a jagged cirque wall, crater, lava field, coast or waterfall.

### Silhouette lock

> A low collapsed bract cone sits beneath two unequal ginger stalk groups and
> one enormous diagonal leaf, while a dense farm fringe closes around one
> narrow upper-right glimpse of a rounded windward slope.

The scene must remain immediately distinguishable from:

- Guadeloupe NIC203's radial torch-ginger flower, S-curving forest trail and
  waterfall slit;

- Pohnpei QG4739's pale ringed palm, open root lattice and steep cloudforest;

### Forbidden

- Cirque interior, amphitheatre wall, lava tongue, crater or erupting volcano.
- Jagged triple-piton skyline.
- Coast, sea, beach, lagoon, waterfall or open water.
- Dominant cane rows or a cane-harvest scene.
