# Manaus-region *Caenorhabditis tropicalis* JU1976 location research and art brief

Last updated: 2026-07-14

## Purpose

This dossier defines the evidence boundary and illustration contract for the
Manaus-region JU1976 environment and its three paired accessories.

## Representative choice

Use JU1976, formerly Br3.1, as the explicit representative.

JU1976 is a CaeNDR isotype-reference strain whose current data agree with the
2011 primary collection table. JU1975 is also a real Brazilian isolate, but its
latest downloadable CaeNDR row contains unresolved Nouragues locality wording
that conflicts with its Brazilian coordinate and date. JU1976 is therefore the
safer public representative.

## Representative collection record

- Species: *Caenorhabditis tropicalis*
- Strain and isotype reference: JU1976
- Previous name: Br3.1
- Coordinates: -2.96, -59.84
- Elevation: 21 m
- Landscape: forest
- Substrate: unidentified rotting fruit
- Locality: Amazon village near Manaus, Brazil
- Collection date: 22 June 2010
- Isolated by: M.-A. Félix
- Collector: unreported
- Nearby isolate: about 4 m from JU1975, which came from a different fruit

The sources do not identify the fruit species, colour, dimensions or anatomy;
whether it was attached or on the ground; its exact decay geometry; the
collection weather; nearby plants or animals; the hydrological forest type;
the direction from JU1975; or the exact property, trail or collection-patch
appearance.

## Current unsupported or misleading implementation

The current scene makes a regional landmark look like the collection habitat.
No collection source connects JU1976 to:

- the Meeting of the Waters;
- the Negro–Solimões confluence;
- a blackwater/whitewater boundary;
- igapó or flooded forest;
- floodplain habitat;
- a canoe;
- visible rain;
- a river- or lake-surface collection.

The Meeting of the Waters is a real feature east of Manaus, but it is
independent regional geography rather than JU1976’s recorded microhabitat. The
exact evidence is forest plus an unidentified rotting fruit.

## Exact-versus-regional evidence boundary

Exact collection evidence:

- JU1976 and alternative name Br3.1
- Coordinates and 21 m elevation
- Forest landscape
- Unidentified rotting fruit
- Amazon village near Manaus
- 22 June 2010
- Isolation by M.-A. Félix
- Approximately 4 m separation from JU1975
- JU1975 and JU1976 came from different fruits

Regional context only:

- Closed humid forest around Manaus
- Low-light understorey and ground-level palm rosettes
- Gently varying plateau, slope and low-position soils
- Clay-rich uplands grading toward sandier lower positions in well-studied
  Manaus-region forest

Official INPA descriptions of Reserva Ducke support those wider regional cues.
They do not show that JU1976 was collected there, and the illustration must not
present a Ducke research grid as the collection site.

## Environment illustration contract

Create a dedicated original SVG scene in the existing 600 × 430 viewBox.

### Camera

- Use a low forest-floor viewpoint.
- Preserve central play space approximately from `x=175–445`, `y=180–350`.
- Include no open river horizon or scenic panorama.

### Foreground

- Place one dominant irregular, collapsed anonymous fruit near the lower left.
- Use muted ochre, brown and restrained berry tones.
- Show shallow compression and soft decay without invented seeds or identifying
  anatomy.
- Place it in irregular leaf litter, explicitly as an illustrative placement.

### Middle

- Bring two unequal ground-level palm fans in from opposite sides.
- Make the left fan low and spreading and the right fan taller and more upright.
- Use a shallow, dry-looking forest-floor contour transitioning subtly from
  deeper ochre soil to a paler lower patch.
- Preserve the central area for the worms and all six movable objects.

### Background

- Build a dense screen of differently spaced straight trunks and narrow lianas.
- Keep the canopy mostly closed.
- Use one slim vertical light opening rather than a ridge or water horizon.
- Include no recognisable building or property.

### Silhouette lock

> An anonymous softened fruit lies below two opposed ground-level palm fans,
> which frame a narrow vertical shaft through a dense screen of tall forest
> trunks.

### Forbidden

- Meeting of the Waters or two-colour water.
- River, lake, broad stream or flooded-root band.
- Igapó presented as the collection habitat.
- Canoe, dock, lodge, bungalow or identifiable private property.
- City skyline.
- Rain curtain or claimed collection weather.
- Named fruit, exposed seeds or reconstructed fruit anatomy.
- Wildlife or claimed fruit association.
- Indigenous or other cultural motifs.
- A Ducke research grid presented as the collection site.

## Accessory contract

### JU1976 substrate-identity shadow theatre

Family ID: `ju1976-substrate-identity-shadow-theatre`

Basis: the specimen was an unidentified rotting fruit. The source mentions a
photograph, but the reviewed public record does not expose one that can support
a reconstruction.

Primary: a broad low optical stage with a frosted central silhouette screen,
irregular covered fruit cradle, three blank rotating outline shutters,
articulated bellows lens, separate culture vial and reversible
`Br3.1 / JU1976` plate. Every hinge and rod must visibly connect.

Companion: a tall asymmetric shadow-box tower with vertical aperture wheel,
sealed lower fruit drawer, offset focusing lens, hanging field-note spool and a
side-mounted `JU1976` tab.

Do not identify the fruit, show invented internal anatomy or turn the companion
into a narrowed primary.

### JU1975–JU1976 four-metre twin-sample rail

Family ID: `ju1975-ju1976-four-metre-sample-rail`

Basis: JU1976 was recorded about 4 m from JU1975, with each isolate coming from
a different fruit.

Primary: a long folding horizontal rail with two structurally unequal covered
sample bays, separate vial cradles, connected measuring tape and winding
handle, `Br2.1` and `Br3.1` end tabs and one restrained `4 m` index.

Companion: a tall helical measuring reel with two offset specimen cages on
separate levels, exposed winding drum and return cable, independent vial drawer
and vertical `4 m` index.

Do not invent direction, exact field alignment or fruit appearance, and do not
imply that both strains came from one specimen.

### Manaus plateau–valley soil harmonograph

Family ID: `manaus-plateau-valley-soil-harmonograph`

Regional basis: official Manaus-region forest descriptions document
plateau-to-lower-position topographic and soil changes. This is educational
regional context, not JU1976 collection equipment.

Primary: a broad stepped cross-section instrument with three connected soil
chambers grading from deep ochre clay to paler sand, a sliding contour stylus,
paired pendulum arms, hand-driven paper drum and open negative space beneath.

Companion: a tall three-drawer gravity column with unequal clay and sand cups,
counterweight, scrolling contour strip, offset pendulum and exposed return
cable.

Do not add numerical soil percentages or claim that the represented gradient
occurred at the collection patch.
